A postpartum client, 24 hours after a vaginal delivery, reports heavy lochia with large clots. The nurse assesses the client and finds the uterus boggy and displaced to the right. Identify the three most likely conditions associated with these findings.

Item type [matrix] rendering in simplified mode.

✓ Clinical Rationales

A boggy uterus indicates uterine atony, a major cause of postpartum hemorrhage because the uterine muscles are not contracting effectively to clamp down on blood vessels at the placental site. Displacement to the right suggests a full bladder, which interferes with uterine contraction by physically preventing the uterus from contracting properly. Heavy lochia with large clots is a sign of excessive bleeding, potentially leading to hypovolemia as the body loses significant blood volume.
