✓ Clinical Rationales

The patient's recent NSTEMI has likely caused left ventricular dysfunction, leading to left-sided heart failure. This is characterized by the backup of blood into the pulmonary circulation, causing pulmonary edema (crackles). As left-sided failure progresses, increased pulmonary pressure strains the right ventricle, leading to right-sided failure. This causes systemic venous congestion, resulting in peripheral edema and JVD. Tachycardia, elevated BNP, and dilutional hyponatremia are systemic responses seen in both types of heart failure.
