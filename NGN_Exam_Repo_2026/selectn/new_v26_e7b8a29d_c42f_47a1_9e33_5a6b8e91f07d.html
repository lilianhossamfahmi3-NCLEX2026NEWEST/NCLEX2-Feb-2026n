Increasing the FiO2 is crucial to improve the patient's hypoxemia (PaO2 65 mmHg, SpO2 90%). Addressing the low PaO2 is paramount. Assessing and managing the patient's pain and agitation is important as agitation can increase oxygen demand and affect ventilation synchrony. The MAR shows Morphine is available PRN but being held due to respiratory rate; alternate pain management should be investigated. Increasing the PEEP can help improve oxygenation by preventing alveolar collapse and increasing the surface area for gas exchange. However, it must be done cautiously, monitoring for barotrauma and decreased cardiac output. Performing endotracheal suctioning is indicated given the coarse crackles and the report of thick secretions obtained during the last suctioning. Clearing the airway of secretions will improve ventilation and oxygenation. **Clinical Pearl:** Always consider the potential complications of each intervention, such as barotrauma with increased PEEP.
