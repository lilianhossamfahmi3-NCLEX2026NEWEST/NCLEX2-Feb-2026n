✓ Clinical Rationales

Implementing strategies to prevent VAP is crucial for mechanically ventilated patients. Elevating the head of the bed to 30-45 degrees minimizes the risk of aspiration by promoting gravitational drainage and reducing reflux. Performing frequent oral care with chlorhexidine reduces the bacterial load in the oral cavity, preventing colonization of the respiratory tract. Ensuring adequate endotracheal cuff pressure (20-30 cm H2O) prevents leakage of contaminated secretions around the endotracheal tube into the lower respiratory tract. Regular suctioning removes secretions that can harbor bacteria, preventing their accumulation and potential aspiration. Minimizing sedation helps facilitate weaning and reduces the duration of mechanical ventilation, a major risk factor for VAP. This is because lighter sedation allows for better cough and gag reflexes, which are protective against aspiration. ClinicalPearl: VAP is defined as pneumonia that develops more than 48 hours after endotracheal intubation.
