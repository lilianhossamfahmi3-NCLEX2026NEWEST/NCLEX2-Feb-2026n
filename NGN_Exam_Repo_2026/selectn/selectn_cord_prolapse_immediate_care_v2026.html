✓ Clinical Rationales

Clinical rationale: In the event of a cord prolapse, the immediate priorities are to relieve pressure on the umbilical cord to restore fetal blood flow and to maximize fetal oxygenation. Applying upward pressure, administering oxygen, and repositioning the mother are the most critical initial steps. Preparing for surgery, monitoring, and fluid administration are important but secondary to these immediate interventions.
