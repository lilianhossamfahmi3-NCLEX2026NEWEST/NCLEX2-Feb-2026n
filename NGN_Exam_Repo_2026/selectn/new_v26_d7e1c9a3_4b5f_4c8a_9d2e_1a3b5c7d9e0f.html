✓ Clinical Rationales

Increasing the IV fluid infusion rate is crucial to address the patient's dehydration and hypotension, which are contributing to decreased urine output and worsening mental status. Rapid fluid replacement helps restore intravascular volume and improve renal perfusion. Administering IV potassium chloride is essential to correct the hypokalemia, which can lead to cardiac arrhythmias and further complications. Insulin shifts potassium intracellularly, so replacement is vital. The insulin infusion rate should be carefully evaluated, but not necessarily decreased immediately, as hyperglycemia still needs to be addressed while cautiously correcting the acidosis and potassium levels. clinicalPearl: Monitor the patient closely for signs of fluid overload during rapid fluid resuscitation, such as crackles in the lungs or jugular venous distension.
