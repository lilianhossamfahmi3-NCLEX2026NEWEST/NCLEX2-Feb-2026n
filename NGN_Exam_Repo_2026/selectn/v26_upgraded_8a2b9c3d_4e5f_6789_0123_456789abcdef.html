✓ Clinical Rationales

The infant is experiencing a hypercyanotic 'Tet' spell, a life-threatening emergency caused by a sudden increase in right-to-left shunting of deoxygenated blood. Immediate interventions must focus on decreasing pulmonary vascular resistance (PVR) and increasing systemic vascular resistance (SVR). Placing the infant in a knee-chest position increases SVR, reducing the shunt. Administering 100% oxygen acts as a pulmonary vasodilator, decreasing PVR. IV morphine sedates the infant, reducing agitation and oxygen demand, and also helps relax the infundibular muscle, decreasing PVR. An IV fluid bolus increases preload and systemic vascular volume, improving cardiac output and pulmonary blood flow.
