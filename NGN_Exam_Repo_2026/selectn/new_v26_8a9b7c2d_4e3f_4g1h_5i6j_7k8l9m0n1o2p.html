✓ Clinical Rationales

Maintaining strict NPO status helps to reduce pancreatic stimulation and allows the inflammation to subside. Administering intravenous fluids helps to correct fluid volume deficit caused by vomiting, third spacing, and decreased oral intake. Monitoring serum electrolytes is crucial because pancreatitis can lead to electrolyte imbalances such as hypokalemia and hyponatremia. Assessing pain level and effectiveness of pain medication is essential for patient comfort and to evaluate the effectiveness of the prescribed analgesics. Monitoring respiratory status is important because acute pancreatitis can lead to complications such as ARDS or pleural effusion.
