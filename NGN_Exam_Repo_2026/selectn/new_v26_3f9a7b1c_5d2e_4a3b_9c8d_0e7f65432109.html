✓ Clinical Rationales

The client is exhibiting signs of severe acute pancreatitis with systemic inflammatory response syndrome (SIRS). The priority interventions focus on stabilizing hemodynamics, reducing pancreatic stimulation, and managing severe symptoms. Aggressive IV fluid resuscitation is critical to combat third-spacing and hypovolemia, preventing shock and acute kidney injury. Maintaining NPO status is essential to allow the pancreas to rest by preventing the release of digestive enzymes. Severe pain requires aggressive IV opioid management. Pancreatic inflammation can impair insulin production, necessitating frequent glucose monitoring. For a client with a distended, rigid abdomen and persistent nausea, an NG tube for decompression can relieve symptoms and reduce pancreatic stimulation.
