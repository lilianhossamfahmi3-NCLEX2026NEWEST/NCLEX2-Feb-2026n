✓ Clinical Rationales

Retinal detachment is a medical emergency requiring prompt diagnosis and treatment to prevent permanent vision loss. The classic symptoms include sudden flashes of light, floaters, and a curtain-like loss of vision. These symptoms result from the separation of the retina from the underlying choroid, disrupting the blood supply and neural connections necessary for sight. Early recognition and intervention are crucial to reattach the retina and restore vision.
