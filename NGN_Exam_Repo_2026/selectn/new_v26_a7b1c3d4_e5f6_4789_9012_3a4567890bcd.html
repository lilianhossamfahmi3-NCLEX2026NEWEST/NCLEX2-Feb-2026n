✓ Clinical Rationales

Administering furosemide intravenously helps reduce fluid overload by promoting diuresis, decreasing preload, and alleviating pulmonary congestion. Increasing oxygen delivery via a non-rebreather mask improves oxygen saturation and alleviates hypoxemia due to pulmonary edema. Elevating the head of the bed to a high Fowler's position promotes lung expansion and decreases venous return, reducing dyspnea. Notifying the provider of the patient's hyperkalemia is essential, as it may require immediate medical intervention to prevent cardiac arrhythmias. Establishing continuous cardiac monitoring is crucial for detecting and managing potential arrhythmias associated with ADHF and hyperkalemia. The elevated BNP supports the diagnosis of heart failure.
