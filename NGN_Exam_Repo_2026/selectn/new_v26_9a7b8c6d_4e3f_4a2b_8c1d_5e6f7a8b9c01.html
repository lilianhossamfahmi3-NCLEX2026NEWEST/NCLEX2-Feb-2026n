✓ Clinical Rationales

Monitoring blood pressure closely is crucial in the postpartum period for clients with preeclampsia because blood pressure can remain elevated or even worsen in the days following delivery. Assessing for signs of postpartum hemorrhage is vital, as preeclampsia can increase the risk of uterine atony and bleeding due to impaired coagulation. Administering labetalol as prescribed for a systolic blood pressure (SBP) greater than 160 mmHg is important to prevent complications such as stroke or seizures.
