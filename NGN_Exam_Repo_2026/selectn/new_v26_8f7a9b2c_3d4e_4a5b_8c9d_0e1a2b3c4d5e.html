✓ Clinical Rationales

Elevating the head of the bed to 30 degrees promotes venous drainage from the brain, reducing ICP. Administering prescribed hyperosmolar medications like mannitol draws fluid from the brain tissue into the vascular space, decreasing ICP. Maintaining a neutral head position prevents obstruction of venous outflow from the brain, which can exacerbate ICP. These actions directly address the Monroe-Kellie doctrine, which states that the total volume of brain, blood, and CSF must remain constant. Reducing any one of these components (in this case, brain tissue fluid) will reduce ICP.
