✓ Clinical Rationales

Autonomic dysreflexia is a life-threatening emergency in clients with spinal cord injuries at T6 or above. A noxious stimulus below the injury level triggers an unopposed sympathetic nervous system surge, causing severe vasoconstriction and hypertension. Baroreceptors detect this hypertension and stimulate a parasympathetic response (via the vagus nerve) above the injury level, but this response is unable to counteract the widespread vasoconstriction below. This leads to the characteristic paradoxical presentation of severe hypertension with bradycardia. The priority interventions are to immediately reduce the blood pressure and eliminate the triggering stimulus.
