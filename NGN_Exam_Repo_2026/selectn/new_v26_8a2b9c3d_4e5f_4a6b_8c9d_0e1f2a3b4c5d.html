✓ Clinical Rationales

Close monitoring of intake and output is crucial to assess for fluid overload and kidney function, as preeclampsia can lead to decreased urine output and fluid retention. Administering acetaminophen for headache PRN is appropriate for managing discomfort; acetaminophen is preferred over NSAIDs due to the risk of increased blood pressure with NSAIDs. Continuing the magnesium sulfate infusion as prescribed is essential to prevent seizures, the primary medication for preventing eclamptic seizures in preeclamptic patients. Hourly assessment of deep tendon reflexes (DTRs) is necessary to monitor for magnesium toxicity; decreased or absent DTRs are an early sign.
