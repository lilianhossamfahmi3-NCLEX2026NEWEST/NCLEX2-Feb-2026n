✓ Clinical Rationales

Hypotension (90/60 mmHg) indicates potential hypovolemic shock due to fluid shifts and third spacing, requiring immediate fluid resuscitation and monitoring. Tachycardia (120 bpm) is a compensatory mechanism for decreased blood pressure and requires investigation to determine the underlying cause (e.g., hypovolemia, pain). Hypoxemia (SpO2 92% on 2L NC) suggests respiratory compromise, potentially due to fluid accumulation in the lungs or decreased respiratory effort from pain. This requires assessment of respiratory status and potential need for increased oxygen support. Low urine output (less than 30 mL/hr, as indicated in the SBAR) indicates inadequate renal perfusion, possibly due to hypovolemia or renal dysfunction, requiring further assessment of fluid status and renal function. Hyponatremia (130 mEq/L), hypokalemia (3.2 mEq/L) and hypocalcemia (7.0 mg/dL) can lead to cardiac arrhythmias and neuromuscular excitability, requiring immediate correction.
