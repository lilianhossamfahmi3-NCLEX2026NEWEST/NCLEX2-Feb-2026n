The charge nurse is reviewing the care plan for a client admitted with septic shock secondary to a urinary tract infection (UTI) caused by a multi-drug resistant organism. Which of the following interventions should the nurse include in the client's plan of care?

Item type [selectN] rendering in simplified mode.

✓ Clinical Rationales

Initiating broad-spectrum antibiotics is crucial in sepsis to target the suspected pathogens promptly, especially given the history of recent antibiotic use and the suspicion of multi-drug resistance. Obtaining blood cultures before administering antibiotics is essential to identify the causative organism and guide antibiotic selection. Monitoring urine output hourly is vital to assess renal perfusion and response to fluid resuscitation in septic shock. Implementing Contact Plus precautions is necessary to prevent the spread of multi-drug resistant organisms to other clients and healthcare personnel.
