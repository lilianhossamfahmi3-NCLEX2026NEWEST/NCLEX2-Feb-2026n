✓ Clinical Rationales

Decreased level of consciousness indicates worsening cerebral edema and increased ICP. Pupillary changes (unequal or sluggish) suggest pressure on the optic nerve (CN II). Hypertension with bradycardia and irregular respirations (Cushing's Triad) is a classic sign of increased ICP, indicating impending brain herniation. These findings necessitate immediate intervention to reduce ICP and prevent further neurological damage.
