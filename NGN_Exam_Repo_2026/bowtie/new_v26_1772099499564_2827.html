✓ Clinical Rationales

Sepsis is characterized by an exaggerated inflammatory response to an infection, leading to widespread vasodilation and capillary leak. This results in hypotension, tachycardia, and often, an elevated white blood cell count (WBC) as the body attempts to fight the infection. Lactic acid levels are also typically elevated due to tissue hypoperfusion. Administering oxygen helps improve tissue oxygenation, counteracting the effects of hypoperfusion. IV fluids are essential to address the vasodilation and capillary leak, helping to restore blood pressure and improve organ perfusion.
