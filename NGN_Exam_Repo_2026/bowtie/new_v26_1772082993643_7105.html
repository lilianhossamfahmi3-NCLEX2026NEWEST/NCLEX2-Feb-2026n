✓ Clinical Rationales

Fluid restriction is indicated to prevent fluid overload, a common complication of AKI. Monitoring intake and output is crucial for assessing fluid balance and kidney function. Restricting potassium intake is important in hyperkalemia, which can occur due to impaired potassium excretion. Avoiding nephrotoxic medications prevents further kidney damage. Dialysis may be required if conservative measures fail to manage fluid overload, hyperkalemia, or metabolic acidosis. Administering oxygen supports tissue perfusion in the setting of potential fluid overload and respiratory compromise. Starting IV fluids may be necessary to address dehydration, a common cause of AKI, but must be carefully managed to avoid fluid overload.
