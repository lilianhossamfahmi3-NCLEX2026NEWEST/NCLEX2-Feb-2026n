✓ Clinical Rationales

The most common causes of acute pancreatitis are gallstone obstruction (c1) and chronic alcohol abuse (c2). Gallstones can block the pancreatic duct, leading to enzyme backup and autodigestion. Chronic alcohol abuse causes pancreatic inflammation and damage over time. Maintaining NPO status (a1) is crucial to reduce pancreatic stimulation and allow it to rest. IV opioid analgesics (a2) are essential for managing the severe pain associated with acute pancreatitis. Monitoring serum amylase and lipase (p1) helps assess the extent of pancreatic damage, as these enzymes are released into the bloodstream during inflammation. Assessing pain level and location (p2) provides valuable information about the patient's response to treatment and potential complications.
