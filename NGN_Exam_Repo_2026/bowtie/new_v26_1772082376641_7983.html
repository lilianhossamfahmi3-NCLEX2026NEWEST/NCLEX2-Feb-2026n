✓ Clinical Rationales

Administer IV fluids: IV fluids help improve renal perfusion and flush out toxins, especially in AKI secondary to dehydration. Adequate hydration supports kidney function. Restrict potassium intake: Hyperkalemia is a common and dangerous complication of AKI. Restricting potassium intake helps prevent or manage hyperkalemia, reducing the risk of cardiac arrhythmias. Monitor electrolyte levels: Electrolyte imbalances are common in AKI and can lead to serious complications. Frequent monitoring allows for timely intervention to prevent adverse outcomes.
