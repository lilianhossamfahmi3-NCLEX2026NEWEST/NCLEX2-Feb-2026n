✓ Clinical Rationales

The client's signs—severe pain unrelieved by opioids, pallor, coolness, and a faint pulse post-ORIF—are classic indicators of acute compartment syndrome. This condition involves increased pressure within a fascial compartment, compromising circulation and nerve function. Priority actions must relieve pressure (remove cast) and secure definitive surgical intervention (notify surgeon).
