✓ Clinical Rationales

Early identification and aggressive management are crucial in sepsis to improve patient outcomes. Measuring lactate levels helps assess tissue perfusion and guides resuscitation efforts. Administering broad-spectrum antibiotics within the first hour of sepsis recognition significantly improves survival rates. Initiating IV fluid resuscitation helps to restore intravascular volume and improve blood pressure in patients with sepsis-induced hypotension. Monitoring urine output is essential to assess the effectiveness of fluid resuscitation and kidney function. Obtaining blood cultures prior to antibiotic administration helps identify the causative organism and guides antibiotic selection. Administering oxygen helps maintain adequate oxygen saturation.
