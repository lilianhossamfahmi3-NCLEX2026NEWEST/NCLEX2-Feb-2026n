The client's presentation of severe hypotension (MAP 60), tachycardia, cool/clammy skin, decreased urine output, and elevated PAWP (22 mmHg) following an MI is classic for cardiogenic shock. This condition is a state of severe cardiac pump failure leading to inadequate tissue perfusion. The primary goals are to improve cardiac output and maintain organ perfusion. Initiating a vasopressor like norepinephrine is critical to raise the MAP to a level (>65 mmHg) that can perfuse vital organs. The client's respiratory distress and hypoxemia indicate impending respiratory failure, making preparation for intubation a priority to secure the airway and reduce the work of breathing.
