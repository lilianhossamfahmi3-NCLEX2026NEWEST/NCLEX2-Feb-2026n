✓ Clinical Rationales

The patient's symptoms (fatigue, weight gain, constipation, cold intolerance, muscle cramps) and lab results (elevated TSH, low Free T4) indicate that the current levothyroxine dosage is inadequate. The nurse should anticipate an order to increase the levothyroxine dosage to achieve euthyroidism. Monitoring for signs of hyperthyroidism (tachycardia, anxiety, insomnia) is crucial after any dosage adjustment. Educating the patient about the signs and symptoms of both hypothyroidism and hyperthyroidism is essential for self-management and early detection of dosage-related issues. Assessing medication adherence is important to rule out non-compliance as a cause of the elevated TSH, although the patient reports adherence.
