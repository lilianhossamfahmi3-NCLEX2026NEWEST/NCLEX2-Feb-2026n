✓ Clinical Rationales

Elevated blood glucose, low pH, decreased bicarbonate, positive serum ketones, Kussmaul respirations, and fruity odor on breath are all indicative of DKA. These findings reflect the body's attempt to compensate for hyperglycemia and metabolic acidosis through increased respiratory rate and ketone production. The presence of ketones in the blood and urine leads to the characteristic fruity odor on the breath.
