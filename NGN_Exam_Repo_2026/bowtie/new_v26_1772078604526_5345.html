✓ Clinical Rationales

Signs of fluid overload include elevated blood pressure, increased heart rate, increased respiratory rate, decreased SpO2, crackles in the lungs, lower extremity edema, jugular venous distention, elevated BNP, and pulmonary congestion on chest X-ray. Interventions to manage fluid overload include administering diuretics (furosemide), restricting fluids, monitoring intake and output, monitoring daily weights, and providing supplemental oxygen. clinicalPearl: In AKI, the kidneys' ability to regulate fluid and electrolyte balance is compromised, making patients susceptible to fluid overload. BNP is a marker of heart failure and is elevated in fluid overload due to increased cardiac workload. Strict adherence to fluid restrictions is crucial in managing fluid overload in AKI patients.
