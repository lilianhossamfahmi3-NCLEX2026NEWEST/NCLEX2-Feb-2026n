✓ Clinical Rationales

DKA is caused by absolute insulin deficiency (c1), often triggered by infection or illness (c2). Initial management priorities are aggressive IV fluid resuscitation with normal saline (a1) to correct dehydration, followed by an IV regular insulin infusion (a2) to correct hyperglycemia and acidosis. Monitoring serum potassium (p1) is critical as insulin drives potassium into cells, and tracking the anion gap (p2) is essential to confirm resolution of the ketoacidosis.
