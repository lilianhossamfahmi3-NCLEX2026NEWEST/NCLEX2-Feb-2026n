✓ Clinical Rationales

The infant's presentation of sudden colicky abdominal pain, drawing knees to the chest, a palpable sausage-shaped mass, and 'currant jelly' stool is the classic presentation for intussusception. The priority actions are to stabilize the client with IV fluids while keeping them NPO and preparing for a non-surgical reduction via an air enema. It is critical to monitor for the passage of a normal brown stool (indicating reduction) and for signs of perforation (fever, rigid abdomen), which is a surgical emergency.
