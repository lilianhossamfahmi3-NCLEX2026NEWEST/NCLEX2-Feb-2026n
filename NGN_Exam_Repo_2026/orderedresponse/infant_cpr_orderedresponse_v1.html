✓ Clinical Rationales

Infant CPR follows the CAB sequence (Circulation, Airway, Breathing). (1) Assess for a brachial pulse and breathing simultaneously for no more than 10 seconds. The brachial pulse is the standard for infants because the carotid artery in the neck is often difficult to palpate accurately. (2) If no pulse or a pulse less than 60 bpm with signs of poor perfusion, begin high-quality chest compressions at a rate of 100-120 compressions per minute, compressing approximately 1.5 inches (4 cm) using the two-finger technique on the sternum, just below the nipple line. (3) Open the airway using a head-tilt/chin-lift maneuver, ensuring a neutral head position to avoid obstructing the airway. Over-extension can collapse the infant's compliant trachea. (4) Deliver 2 rescue breaths, ensuring a tight seal over both the infant's nose and mouth to prevent air leakage. Each breath should be delivered over 1 second, with enough volume to see the chest rise. (5) Continue chest compressions and rescue breaths in a 30:2 ratio for approximately 2 minutes (about 5 cycles) before reassessing the infant's condition or using an AED if available. This ensures consistent circulation and oxygenation.
