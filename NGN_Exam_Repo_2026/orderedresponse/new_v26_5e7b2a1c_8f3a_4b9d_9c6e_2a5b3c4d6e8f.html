✓ Clinical Rationales

The correct sequence prioritizes immediate, non-invasive actions to lower the critically high blood pressure and then systematically removes the underlying cause. Autonomic dysreflexia is a medical emergency triggered by a noxious stimulus below the level of a T6 or higher spinal cord injury. The stimulus causes a massive sympathetic response, leading to vasoconstriction and severe hypertension below the injury. The parasympathetic system attempts to compensate by slowing the heart rate (bradycardia) and causing vasodilation above the injury, but this response is inadequate. Elevating the head of the bed is the first priority as it uses gravity (orthostatic hypotension) to help lower blood pressure. Simultaneously removing constrictive items and then addressing the most common trigger (bladder distention) is key to resolving the cause. If these measures fail, pharmacological intervention is necessary, followed by notification of the provider.
