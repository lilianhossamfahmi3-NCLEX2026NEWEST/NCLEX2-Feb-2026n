✓ Clinical Rationales

Maintaining adequate cerebral perfusion pressure (CPP) and addressing Cushing's triad are critical in managing increased ICP. Positioning to promote venous drainage is the initial step to reduce ICP. Administering prescribed medications such as mannitol helps reduce cerebral edema. Hyperventilating the patient can acutely lower PaCO2, causing vasoconstriction and reducing ICP, but it requires careful monitoring and is typically a later intervention. Finally, notifying the healthcare provider is essential to inform them of the changes and implement further interventions.
