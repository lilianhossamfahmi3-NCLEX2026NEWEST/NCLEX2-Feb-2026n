✓ Clinical Rationales

DKA management prioritizes addressing life-threatening issues first. Fluid resuscitation with isotonic saline (0.9% NaCl) is crucial to correct dehydration and hypovolemia caused by osmotic diuresis. Insulin administration follows to address hyperglycemia and ketogenesis. Potassium replacement is essential to prevent hypokalemia due to insulin's intracellular shift of potassium. Finally, identifying the underlying cause (e.g., infection, missed insulin dose) is important to prevent future episodes.
