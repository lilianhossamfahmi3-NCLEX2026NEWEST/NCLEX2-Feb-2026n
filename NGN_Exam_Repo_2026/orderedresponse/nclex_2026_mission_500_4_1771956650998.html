A patient with Acute Respiratory Distress Syndrome (ARDS) is being mechanically ventilated. The following ventilator settings are prescribed by the healthcare provider. Prioritize the order in which the nurse should implement these interventions.

Item type [orderedResponse] rendering in simplified mode.

✓ Clinical Rationales

1. **Assess breath sounds and chest excursion:** This is the initial step to establish a baseline and identify any immediate issues like pneumothorax or asymmetrical breathing, which can quickly become life-threatening. Assessing provides a foundation for subsequent interventions. 2. **Evaluate arterial blood gas (ABG) results:** ABGs provide crucial information about the patient's oxygenation, ventilation, and acid-base balance. Evaluating ABGs allows for targeted adjustments to ventilator settings. In ARDS, it's critical to assess PaO2, PaCO2, and pH to guide further interventions. This step informs the necessary adjustments to improve respiratory function. 3. **Adjust FiO2 to maintain SpO2 between 88-92%:** Maintaining adequate oxygen saturation is vital. The target SpO2 range of 88-92% is often used in ARDS to balance oxygen delivery with the risk of oxygen toxicity. Titrating FiO2 is essential to achieve this balance. Aiming for lower SpO2 can prevent hyperoxia and related complications. This adjustment optimizes oxygen delivery while minimizing potential harm. 4. **Increase positive end-expiratory pressure (PEEP) as prescribed:** PEEP helps to keep alveoli open and improves gas exchange by preventing alveolar collapse at the end of expiration. Increasing PEEP can improve oxygenation, but it must be done cautiously to avoid barotrauma. Higher PEEP can recruit more alveoli, improving oxygenation. Careful titration is essential to maximize benefit and minimize risk. 5. **Administer prescribed sedative medication:** Sedation is typically administered after ensuring adequate ventilation and oxygenation. Sedatives reduce patient anxiety and promote synchrony with the ventilator, which in turn improves the effectiveness of mechanical ventilation. This is essential for comfort but should come after ensuring respiratory stability. Sedation facilitates ventilator synchrony and patient comfort once respiratory parameters are optimized.
