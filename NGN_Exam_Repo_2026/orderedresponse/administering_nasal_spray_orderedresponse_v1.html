✓ Clinical Rationales

Correct nasal spray technique maximizes medication delivery to the nasal mucosa for optimal therapeutic effect. (1) **Blow the nose gently to clear the nasal passages:** This removes mucus and debris, allowing the medication to directly contact the nasal mucosa for absorption. Mucus acts as a barrier, hindering drug absorption and reducing efficacy. (2) **Tilt the head forward slightly and insert the bottle tip into one nostril:** Tilting the head forward prevents the medication from draining down the throat, which can lead to systemic absorption and reduced local effect. Inserting the tip ensures targeted delivery to the nasal passages. (3) **Close the other nostril with a finger:** Occluding one nostril ensures that the medication is inhaled solely through the nostril receiving the spray, maximizing drug concentration in that nasal passage. (4) **Inhale through the nose while firmly depressing the spray bottle:** Simultaneous inhalation and spray administration ensures the medication is drawn deep into the nasal passages, reaching the target tissues. Firm depression delivers the correct dose. (5) **Exhale through the mouth and avoid blowing the nose for several minutes:** Exhaling through the mouth prevents the medication from being expelled from the nasal passages. Avoiding blowing the nose allows the medication to remain in contact with the nasal mucosa for optimal absorption.
