✓ Clinical Rationales

The correct sequence for managing HHS prioritizes the most immediate life threats. The first priority is correcting profound hypovolemia with aggressive IV fluids to prevent circulatory collapse. The second is initiating cardiac monitoring due to critical hypokalemia, which will be exacerbated by treatment. Third, potassium must be replaced *before* starting insulin to prevent life-threatening dysrhythmias. Fourth, once volume and potassium are addressed, a slow insulin infusion is started to gradually lower blood glucose. Finally, continuous neurological monitoring is essential to detect cerebral edema, a potential complication of rapid fluid and osmolarity shifts.
