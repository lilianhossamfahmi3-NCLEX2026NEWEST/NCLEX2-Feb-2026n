✓ Clinical Rationales

External fetal monitoring is a non-invasive method used to assess fetal well-being during labor by tracking the fetal heart rate (FHR) and uterine contractions. **(1) Perform Leopold maneuvers:** Leopold maneuvers are a systematic way to palpate the maternal abdomen to determine fetal presentation, position, and lie. This step is crucial for identifying the optimal location for the ultrasound transducer to obtain a clear FHR signal. Knowing the fetal position helps in accurately placing the transducer over the fetal back where the heart sounds are best auscultated. **(2) Apply conducting gel to the ultrasound (US) transducer:** Applying gel to the transducer ensures good contact between the transducer and the maternal skin. The gel acts as a conductive medium, facilitating the transmission of ultrasound waves and improving the quality of the FHR signal. **(3) Place the US transducer over the area corresponding to the fetal back:** The ultrasound transducer emits and receives ultrasound waves that bounce off the fetal heart, allowing the monitor to calculate the FHR. Placing the transducer over the fetal back maximizes the signal strength and clarity, providing the most accurate FHR reading. The fetal back is typically located after performing Leopold maneuvers. **(4) Place the tocotransducer (toco) over the uterine fundus:** The tocotransducer measures uterine contractions. It is placed over the fundus because the fundus is the most muscular part of the uterus and where contractions are most easily detected. The toco measures the frequency and duration of contractions but not their intensity. **(5) Adjust the belts and verify that the FHR is distinct from the maternal pulse:** After placing both transducers, the belts are adjusted to ensure they are snug but not too tight, allowing for continuous monitoring without causing discomfort. It is essential to differentiate the FHR from the maternal heart rate to avoid misinterpretation of the data. This can be done by palpating the maternal pulse while simultaneously listening to the FHR. A significant difference in rate confirms that the FHR is being accurately monitored.
