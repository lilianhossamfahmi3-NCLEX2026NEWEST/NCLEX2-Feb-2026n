✓ Clinical Rationales

The correct doffing sequence prioritizes removing the most contaminated items first while protecting the airway. (1) **Gloves:** These are the most contaminated and should be removed first to prevent further spread of pathogens. (2) **Goggles/Face Shield:** These protect the eyes and face from splashes or sprays. Remove carefully to avoid self-contamination. (3) **Gown:** Unfasten the gown and pull it away from the body, rolling it inside out to contain contaminants. This prevents inhalation of any residual airborne particles. Removing the N95 inside the room could expose the nurse to these pathogens, leading to infection. The closed door minimizes airflow and potential for airborne particles to escape.
