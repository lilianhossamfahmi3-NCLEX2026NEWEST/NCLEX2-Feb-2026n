✓ Clinical Rationales

The correct order ensures accurate peak expiratory flow (PEF) measurement, a key indicator of airway obstruction in asthma. * **Step 1:** The indicator must be at zero to provide a baseline for measurement. * **Step 2:** A deep breath maximizes the volume of air available for exhalation, ensuring an accurate PEF reading. This deep inspiration increases intrathoracic pressure, which is then forcefully expelled. * **Step 3:** A tight seal around the mouthpiece prevents air leaks, which would falsely lower the PEF reading. Air leaks reduce the pressure generated during exhalation. * **Step 4:** A forceful and rapid exhalation generates the PEF, reflecting the maximum speed of airflow. This measures the degree of airway narrowing. * **Step 5:** Recording the highest of three readings provides the most accurate representation of the client's peak expiratory flow. Multiple attempts account for variability in effort and technique.
