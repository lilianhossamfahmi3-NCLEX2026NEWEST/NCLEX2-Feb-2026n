✓ Clinical Rationales

The patient is exhibiting signs of AKI superimposed on CKD exacerbation. The elevated creatinine and BUN, along with a low GFR, point to impaired kidney function. The recent missed dialysis and minimal urine output exacerbate the situation. The respiratory distress and low SpO2 are likely due to fluid overload secondary to kidney dysfunction. Hyperkalemia is an electrolyte emergency commonly seen in renal failure. The ABG reveals metabolic acidosis, a common complication of kidney disease. Immediate interventions: - For AKI/CKD: Administer prescribed diuretics (furosemide) to manage fluid overload contributing to respiratory distress and edema. Continuous monitoring of respiratory status and oxygen saturation is crucial. - For Hyperkalemia: Initiate continuous cardiac monitoring and administer medications to lower potassium levels, such as calcium gluconate to protect the heart, insulin and dextrose to shift potassium intracellularly, and consider sodium polystyrene sulfonate (Kayexalate) to remove potassium from the body. A stat EKG should be obtained to assess for cardiac changes related to hyperkalemia. - For Metabolic Acidosis: Address the underlying cause (renal failure). Bicarbonate administration may be considered, but caution is required to avoid fluid overload. Monitor ABGs closely to assess the effectiveness of interventions.
