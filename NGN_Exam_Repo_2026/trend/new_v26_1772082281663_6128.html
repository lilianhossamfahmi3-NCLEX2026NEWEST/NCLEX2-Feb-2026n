✓ Clinical Rationales

Respiratory rate of 10 breaths per minute indicates potential magnesium toxicity and respiratory depression. Magnesium sulfate is a central nervous system depressant, and respiratory depression is a serious adverse effect. Immediate intervention is required to prevent respiratory arrest. The therapeutic range for magnesium sulfate is typically 4-7 mEq/L (approximately 4.8-8.4 mg/dL), although lab values can vary slightly. This patient's magnesium level may be within range, but signs of toxicity can appear even within range.
