✓ Clinical Rationales

Autonomic dysreflexia is a medical emergency characterized by a sudden onset of excessively high blood pressure. The triggering stimulus is usually related to a noxious stimulus below the level of the spinal cord injury. Key interventions include elevating the head of the bed to reduce blood pressure, identifying and removing the triggering stimulus (e.g., bladder distention, bowel impaction), monitoring blood pressure closely, and administering antihypertensive medications as prescribed. Failing to address the underlying cause can lead to severe complications such as stroke or seizure. The nurse must act quickly and systematically to manage the patient's condition.
