✓ Clinical Rationales

Autonomic dysreflexia is a medical emergency requiring immediate intervention to prevent stroke, seizure, or death. Elevating the head of the bed is the initial action to reduce blood pressure by promoting venous pooling. Identifying and removing the cause of the irritation is crucial; common causes include bladder distension or bowel impaction. Checking the Foley catheter for kinks or obstruction addresses bladder distension. Administering antihypertensive medication is the next step if the blood pressure remains elevated after addressing the potential cause. Documenting the interventions and patient response is essential for ongoing care and communication. clinicalPearl: Rapid identification and management of autonomic dysreflexia are critical to prevent severe complications.
