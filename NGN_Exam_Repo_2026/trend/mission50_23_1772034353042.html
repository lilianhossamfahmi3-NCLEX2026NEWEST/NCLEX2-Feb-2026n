✓ Clinical Rationales

Cushing's triad is a classic but late sign of increased ICP and consists of hypertension with a widening pulse pressure, bradycardia, and irregular respirations. The body attempts to maintain cerebral perfusion by increasing blood pressure. The increased pressure stimulates the baroreceptors, leading to a reflex bradycardia. Changes in respiratory pattern occur as ICP affects the brainstem.
