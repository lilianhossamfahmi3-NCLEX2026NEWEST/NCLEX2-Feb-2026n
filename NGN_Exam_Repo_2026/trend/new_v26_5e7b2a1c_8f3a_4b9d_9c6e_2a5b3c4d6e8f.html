✓ Clinical Rationales

Autonomic dysreflexia (AD) is a medical emergency requiring immediate intervention. First, elevate the head of bed to at least 45 degrees to reduce blood pressure. Then, assess for and remove the triggering stimulus, beginning with the most common cause: bladder distension. Catheterize the patient to relieve any urinary retention. Monitor blood pressure closely during and after interventions. If BP remains elevated after addressing bladder issues, administer antihypertensive medication (nifedipine) as prescribed. Continuously reassess the patient's symptoms and BP.
