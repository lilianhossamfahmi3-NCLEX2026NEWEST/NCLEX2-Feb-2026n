✓ Clinical Rationales

The patient's presentation – elevated temperature, tachycardia, hypertension, altered mental status, elevated free T3 and T4, suppressed TSH, and hyperglycemia – strongly suggests thyroid storm. The interventions aim to reduce thyroid hormone synthesis and release, block the effects of thyroid hormones on the body, control symptoms, and address precipitating factors. Administering PTU or methimazole blocks thyroid hormone synthesis. Administering beta-blockers like propranolol or esmolol helps control tachycardia, hypertension, and anxiety by blocking the effects of thyroid hormones on beta-adrenergic receptors. Administering IV fluids addresses dehydration and supports cardiovascular function. Applying cooling blankets helps reduce fever and metabolic demands. Monitoring cardiac rhythm is crucial to detect and manage arrhythmias, a common complication of thyroid storm. clinicalPearl: Remember the '5 B's' of thyroid storm treatment: Block synthesis (PTU/methimazole), Block release (iodine), Block T4 to T3 conversion (PTU/propranolol), Beta-blockers (propranolol/esmolol), and Block enterohepatic circulation (cholestyramine).
