The nurse is caring for a patient three days post-operative from an open reduction and internal fixation (ORIF) of the tibia. Which assessment findings, if any, indicate the development of compartment syndrome? For each finding, indicate if it suggests a positive or negative finding for compartment syndrome.

Item type [trend] rendering in simplified mode.

✓ Clinical Rationales

Compartment syndrome is characterized by the '6 Ps': pain (especially with passive movement), pallor, paresthesia, paralysis, pulselessness, and poikilothermia. Pain out of proportion to the injury and unrelieved by analgesics is a key early sign. Paresthesia is also an early finding. Pulselessness is a late sign, as is paralysis. Capillary refill is not a reliable indicator. Increased swelling contributes to increased pressure within the compartment.
