Overlapping the IV insulin with the first subQ dose for 1-2 hours is standard practice to prevent rebound hyperglycemia. *Pathophysiology Rationale:* DKA results from insulin deficiency, leading to hyperglycemia, osmotic diuresis, and breakdown of fatty acids into ketones. As insulin is administered, glucose is utilized, and ketone production decreases. The goal is to normalize pH and bicarbonate levels while preventing hypoglycemia. Once these parameters are met, transitioning to subcutaneous insulin allows for continued glucose control without the risks associated with continuous IV infusions.
