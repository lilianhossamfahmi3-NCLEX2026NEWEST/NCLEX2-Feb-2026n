✓ Clinical Rationales

Potassium of 5.8 mEq/L. Hyperkalemia is a life-threatening complication of DKA. Although total body potassium is often depleted, serum potassium may be elevated due to insulin deficiency and acidosis, which causes potassium to shift out of cells. Cardiac arrhythmias can result from hyperkalemia, requiring immediate intervention such as calcium gluconate, insulin and glucose administration, or Kayexalate.
