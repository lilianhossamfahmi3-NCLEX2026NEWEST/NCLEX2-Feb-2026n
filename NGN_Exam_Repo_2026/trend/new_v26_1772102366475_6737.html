✓ Clinical Rationales

A serum sodium level of 155 mEq/L is critically high and indicates severe dehydration and hypernatremia, requiring immediate intervention to prevent neurological complications such as seizures or coma. The normal range for serum sodium is typically 135-145 mEq/L. In diabetes insipidus, the kidneys are unable to concentrate urine due to a deficiency of antidiuretic hormone (ADH) or resistance to ADH, leading to excessive water loss and subsequent hypernatremia if fluid replacement is inadequate. Immediate interventions include administering hypotonic intravenous fluids (e.g., 0.45% sodium chloride) to gradually correct the sodium imbalance and monitoring neurological status closely.
