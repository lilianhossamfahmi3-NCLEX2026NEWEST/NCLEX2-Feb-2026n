✓ Clinical Rationales

Epinephrine is the first-line treatment for anaphylaxis due to its ability to reverse the effects of histamine and other mediators released during an allergic reaction. It causes vasoconstriction, increasing blood pressure; bronchodilation, improving breathing; and reduces angioedema and urticaria. Given the patient's rapidly deteriorating condition with airway compromise (stridor, angioedema, SpO2 88%), administering epinephrine is the priority to prevent further deterioration and potential respiratory arrest. The IM route is preferred in this acute setting for rapid absorption.
