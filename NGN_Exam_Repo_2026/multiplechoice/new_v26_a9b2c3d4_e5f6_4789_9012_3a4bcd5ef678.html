✓ Clinical Rationales

The first and most crucial intervention is to elevate the head of the bed to 30 degrees while keeping the head in a neutral, midline position. This action uses gravity to promote venous drainage from the brain via the jugular veins, which decreases cerebral blood volume and can immediately lower intracranial pressure. It is a rapid, non-pharmacological, and foundational intervention for managing increased ICP.
