✓ Clinical Rationales

During a hypercyanotic spell, systemic vascular resistance drops, and pulmonary vascular resistance increases, causing a profound right-to-left shunt of deoxygenated blood. This leads to severe hypoxemia and peripheral vasoconstriction. Administering medication via the subcutaneous route is incorrect because the compromised peripheral perfusion will lead to slow, erratic, and unreliable absorption. For a life-threatening event like a 'tet spell', medications must be given via a route that ensures rapid onset, such as intravenous (IV) or intramuscular (IM).
