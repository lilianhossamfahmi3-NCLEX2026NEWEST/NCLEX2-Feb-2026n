✓ Clinical Rationales

Heparin-induced thrombocytopenia (HIT) is a potentially life-threatening condition characterized by a significant drop in platelet count following heparin administration. The immediate priority is to discontinue the heparin infusion to prevent further platelet activation and thrombosis. Delaying this action could lead to severe thromboembolic complications such as DVT, pulmonary embolism, or stroke. Notifying the provider is crucial for further management, including ordering HIT antibody testing and initiating alternative anticoagulation.
