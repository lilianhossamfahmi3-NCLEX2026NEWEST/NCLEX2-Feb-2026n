✓ Clinical Rationales

The client's presentation of a rapid GCS decline to 8, a new fixed and dilated pupil, and Cushing's triad (hypertension, bradycardia, irregular respirations) are classic signs of dangerously increased intracranial pressure (ICP) and impending brain herniation. The priority is to take immediate action to reduce ICP. The care plan includes a PRN order for 3% hypertonic saline for these exact signs. This hyperosmolar therapy creates an osmotic gradient, drawing water out of the edematous brain tissue and into the vasculature, thereby rapidly decreasing ICP and preventing further neurological injury.
