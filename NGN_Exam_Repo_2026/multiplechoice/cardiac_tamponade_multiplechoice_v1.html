✓ Clinical Rationales

Pulsus paradoxus is the most definitive bedside finding for cardiac tamponade. It reflects the exaggerated drop in systolic blood pressure during inspiration due to the restricted ventricular filling caused by the pericardial fluid. While other findings like JVD, muffled heart sounds, and hypotension are suggestive, pulsus paradoxus provides more specific confirmation.
