✓ Clinical Rationales

The Parkland formula is 4 mL x % TBSA x body weight (kg). In this case, 4 mL x 40 x 70 kg = 11,200 mL or 11.2 L over 24 hours. Half of this amount (5.6 L) should be administered in the first 8 hours from the time of the burn. This rapid infusion is critical to maintain adequate circulating volume and prevent hypovolemic shock in burn patients due to massive fluid shifts and capillary leak. Early and aggressive fluid resuscitation is essential for maintaining organ perfusion and preventing complications associated with burn shock.
