✓ Clinical Rationales

The neonate exhibits significant respiratory distress signs: cyanosis, intercostal retractions, nasal flaring, grunting, and low oxygen saturation (85%). Coupled with meconium-stained amniotic fluid and low initial Apgar scores, Meconium Aspiration Syndrome (MAS) is highly probable. MAS causes airway obstruction, inflammation, and surfactant dysfunction, impairing gas exchange and causing hypoxemia. The FIRST and most critical action is initiating positive pressure ventilation (PPV) via bag-valve-mask for immediate ventilatory support and improved oxygenation. PPV opens alveoli, clears airways, and reduces breathing effort. Delaying PPV can lead to deterioration, hypoxia, and potential brain damage.
