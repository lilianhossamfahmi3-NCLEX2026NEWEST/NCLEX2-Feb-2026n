✓ Clinical Rationales

Compartment syndrome is a surgical emergency. The increased pressure within a limited space compromises circulation and nerve function. A fasciotomy is required to relieve the pressure and prevent permanent damage, including tissue necrosis, nerve damage, and amputation. The nurse must act quickly to prepare for this procedure to minimize potential complications.
