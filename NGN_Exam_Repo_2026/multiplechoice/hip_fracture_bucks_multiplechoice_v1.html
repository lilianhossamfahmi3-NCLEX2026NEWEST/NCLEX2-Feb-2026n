✓ Clinical Rationales

Maintaining the effectiveness of Buck's traction is crucial for reducing muscle spasms and pain in a patient with a hip fracture awaiting surgery. The traction works by applying a continuous pulling force to the affected limb, which is achieved through the freely hanging weight. If the weight is resting on the floor, this force is lost, leading to increased pain, muscle spasms, and potential malalignment of the fracture. Therefore, ensuring the weight hangs freely is the priority to maintain the therapeutic effect of the traction.
