✓ Clinical Rationales

Tension pneumothorax is a life-threatening condition requiring immediate intervention to relieve pressure in the pleural space. Needle thoracostomy is the immediate priority to decompress the affected side, converting the tension pneumothorax into a simple pneumothorax, allowing for improved ventilation and perfusion. Delaying this intervention can lead to cardiovascular collapse and death. The classic signs of tracheal deviation (away from the affected side), acute respiratory distress, and diminished breath sounds on the affected side are indicative of this emergency. The nurse should prepare for needle thoracostomy, which may include gathering supplies and assisting the provider.
