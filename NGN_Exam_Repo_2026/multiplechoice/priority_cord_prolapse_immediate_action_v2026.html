✓ Clinical Rationales

The priority action in the event of a prolapsed umbilical cord is to relieve pressure on the cord to restore fetal oxygenation. Inserting two gloved fingers into the vagina and elevating the presenting part (fetal head or buttocks) off the umbilical cord is the most immediate and effective way to accomplish this. This action reduces cord compression, allowing blood flow and oxygen delivery to the fetus to resume. Maintaining this position is crucial until the baby is delivered via cesarean section. Failure to act immediately can result in fetal hypoxia and potential neurological damage or fetal demise.
