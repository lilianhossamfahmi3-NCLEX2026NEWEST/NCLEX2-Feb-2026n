✓ Clinical Rationales

Stopping the transfusion immediately is the priority action when a hemolytic transfusion reaction is suspected. This prevents further exposure to the incompatible blood, which can lead to severe complications such as disseminated intravascular coagulation (DIC) and acute kidney injury. Disconnecting the IV tubing prevents any residual blood from entering the patient's circulation. Immediate intervention is crucial to minimize the severity of the reaction and potentially life-threatening consequences.
