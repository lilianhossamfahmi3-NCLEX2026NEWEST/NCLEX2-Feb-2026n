✓ Clinical Rationales

Septic shock is a life-threatening condition characterized by persistent hypotension despite adequate fluid resuscitation. A Mean Arterial Pressure (MAP) of 63 mmHg is below the goal of ≥65 mmHg, indicating inadequate organ and tissue perfusion. This profound vasodilation and circulatory collapse is the hallmark pathophysiological sign that sepsis has progressed to septic shock.
