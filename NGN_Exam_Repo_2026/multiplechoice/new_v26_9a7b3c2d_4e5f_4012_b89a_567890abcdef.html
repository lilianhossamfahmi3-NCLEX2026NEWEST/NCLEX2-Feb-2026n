✓ Clinical Rationales

The most significant indicator of a positive response is the improvement in blood pressure to 120/78 mmHg (MAP 92). HHS causes profound dehydration and hypovolemia, leading to hypotension and circulatory collapse. Correcting this with aggressive IV fluid resuscitation is the primary life-saving intervention. A normalizing blood pressure directly reflects successful restoration of intravascular volume and improved tissue perfusion.
