✓ Clinical Rationales

Autonomic dysreflexia is a medical emergency characterized by an abrupt onset of excessively high blood pressure, bradycardia, and other autonomic symptoms. The FIRST action is to elevate the head of the bed to 90 degrees to promote orthostatic hypotension and reduce blood pressure. Loosening tight clothing can also help reduce external stimuli that may be exacerbating the condition. This immediate intervention aims to decrease the patient's blood pressure rapidly and prevent complications such as stroke or seizure.
