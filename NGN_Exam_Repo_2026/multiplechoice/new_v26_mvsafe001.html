✓ Clinical Rationales

Elevating the head of the bed to 30-45 degrees reduces the risk of aspiration and subsequent pneumonia. Performing regular oral care with chlorhexidine reduces the bacterial load in the mouth, decreasing the risk of VAE. Ensuring adequate endotracheal tube cuff pressure (20-30 cm H2O) prevents leakage of secretions into the lower respiratory tract. Assessing readiness to wean daily helps to reduce the overall duration of mechanical ventilation and, consequently, the risk of VAE. Regular suctioning of secretions, especially when indicated by increased respiratory rate or adventitious breath sounds, clears the airway and prevents mucus plugging and potential infection.
