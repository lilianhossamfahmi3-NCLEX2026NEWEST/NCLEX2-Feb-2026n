The client is exhibiting Cushing's triad, a classic sign of increased intracranial pressure (ICP). The triad consists of hypertension (widening pulse pressure), bradycardia, and irregular respirations. The priority is to immediately notify the healthcare provider about these findings, as they indicate a potentially life-threatening situation requiring urgent intervention to reduce ICP and prevent further brain damage. Rapid intervention is crucial to improve patient outcomes.
