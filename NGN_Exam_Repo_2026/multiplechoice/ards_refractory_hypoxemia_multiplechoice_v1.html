✓ Clinical Rationales

The patient's presentation of sudden respiratory distress and refractory hypoxemia (PaO2 of 55 mmHg despite 100% FiO2) in the context of septic shock strongly suggests ARDS. Sepsis is a common trigger for ARDS, leading to alveolar-capillary membrane damage and intrapulmonary shunting, which causes hypoxemia that is unresponsive to supplemental oxygen. The other options are less likely given the refractory nature of the hypoxemia and the patient's history of septic shock.
