The immediate priority is administering labetalol to lower the client's blood pressure. After alteplase administration, uncontrolled hypertension (SBP > 180 mmHg) is a critical risk factor for hemorrhagic conversion. The thrombolytic agent dissolves the clot but also creates a fragile vascular environment. High pressure against these vessels can lead to rupture and life-threatening intracranial bleeding. The standing order provides a clear parameter for intervention, and the client's current BP of 180/100 mmHg meets this criterion.
