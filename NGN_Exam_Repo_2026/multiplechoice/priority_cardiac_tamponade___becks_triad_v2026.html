✓ Clinical Rationales

The patient is exhibiting signs of cardiac tamponade, including hypotension, muffled heart sounds, and jugular venous distension (Beck's triad), along with pulsus paradoxus. Cardiac tamponade is a life-threatening condition requiring immediate intervention to relieve pressure on the heart. Pericardiocentesis is the definitive treatment to remove fluid from the pericardial sac, improving cardiac output and preventing circulatory collapse. While other actions are important, preparing for pericardiocentesis is the priority.
