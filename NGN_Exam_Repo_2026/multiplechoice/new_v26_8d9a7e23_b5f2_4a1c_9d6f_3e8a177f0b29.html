✓ Clinical Rationales

The highest priority is monitoring for signs of hypocalcemia. In acute pancreatitis, severe inflammation and enzymatic fat necrosis occur. Free fatty acids are released and bind with calcium, a process called saponification, which depletes serum calcium levels. Hypocalcemia can lead to life-threatening complications, including tetany, seizures, and cardiac dysrhythmias (prolonged QT interval). The client's lab results confirm a low calcium level of 7.8 mg/dL, making this monitoring essential.
