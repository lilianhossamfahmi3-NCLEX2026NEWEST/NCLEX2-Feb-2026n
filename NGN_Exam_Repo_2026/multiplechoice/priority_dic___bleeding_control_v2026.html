✓ Clinical Rationales

The patient's lab values (prolonged PT/PTT, elevated D-dimer, decreased fibrinogen and platelets) combined with the oozing from sites and petechiae strongly suggest Disseminated Intravascular Coagulation (DIC). DIC is a life-threatening condition characterized by abnormal activation of the coagulation cascade, leading to widespread clotting and subsequent depletion of clotting factors and platelets, resulting in bleeding. The priority action is to administer platelets to address the thrombocytopenia and support clotting. This directly addresses the immediate threat of hemorrhage. The provider order is already in place to administer platelets.
