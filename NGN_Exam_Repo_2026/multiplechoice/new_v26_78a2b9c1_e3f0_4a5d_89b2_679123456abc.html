✓ Clinical Rationales

Diminished or absent breath sounds on one side in a mechanically ventilated client is the most critical finding, strongly indicating a life-threatening complication such as a tension pneumothorax. Positive pressure from the ventilator forces air into the pleural space, causing the lung to collapse. This leads to acute hypoxia, hemodynamic instability, and mediastinal shift (evidenced by tracheal deviation), which can rapidly progress to cardiac arrest if not treated immediately.
