✓ Clinical Rationales

The patient's reported tingling and muscle twitching are indicative of potential hypocalcemia, a common complication following thyroidectomy due to potential damage to the parathyroid glands. Inflating a blood pressure cuff on the arm for 3 minutes assesses for Trousseau's sign, a specific indicator of hypocalcemia. A positive Trousseau's sign (carpal spasm) confirms the suspicion and warrants further investigation and treatment.
