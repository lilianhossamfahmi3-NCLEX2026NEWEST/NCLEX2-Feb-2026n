✓ Clinical Rationales

The first and most critical nursing action for autonomic dysreflexia is to place the client in a high-Fowler's position (sitting upright at 90 degrees). This intervention uses orthostatic hypotension to its advantage, causing blood to pool in the lower extremities and immediately helping to reduce the dangerously high blood pressure. This is a rapid, non-pharmacological intervention that can prevent a hypertensive crisis, stroke, or seizure.
