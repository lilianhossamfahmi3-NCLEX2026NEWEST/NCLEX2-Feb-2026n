✓ Clinical Rationales

Late decelerations indicate uteroplacental insufficiency and potential fetal hypoxia. Administering oxygen and repositioning the mother to a left lateral position are immediate interventions to improve oxygen delivery to the fetus. Oxygen increases maternal oxygen saturation, increasing oxygen availability to the fetus. Left lateral positioning reduces pressure on the vena cava, improving venous return and increasing cardiac output, thereby enhancing placental perfusion.
