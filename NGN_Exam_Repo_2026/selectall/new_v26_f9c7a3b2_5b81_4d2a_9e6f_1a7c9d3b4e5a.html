✓ Clinical Rationales

The correct interventions for ARDS focus on the 'lung-protective' strategy. This involves using low tidal volumes to prevent volutrauma, maintaining adequate PEEP to prevent atelectrauma (alveolar collapse), and improving ventilation-perfusion (V/Q) matching through prone positioning. Frequent ABG monitoring is essential to titrate these therapies effectively. For severe ARDS with ventilator dyssynchrony, NMBAs are used after adequate sedation to reduce oxygen consumption and allow the ventilator to control breathing, preventing further lung injury.
