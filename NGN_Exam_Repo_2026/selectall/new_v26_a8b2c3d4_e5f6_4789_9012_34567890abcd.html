✓ Clinical Rationales

The correct interventions for autonomic dysreflexia (AD) prioritize identifying and eliminating the noxious stimulus causing the massive, uninhibited sympathetic nervous system response below the level of injury. The first action is to elevate the head of the bed to induce orthostatic hypotension and lower cerebral pressure. Concurrently, the nurse must assess for the most common triggers, bladder and bowel distention, and remove any constrictive clothing. Removing the stimulus (e.g., by catheterization) is the definitive treatment. Continuous blood pressure monitoring is essential to gauge the response to these interventions.
