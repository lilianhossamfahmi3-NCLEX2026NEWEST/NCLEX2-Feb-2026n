✓ Clinical Rationales

Elevate the head of the bed to at least 45 degrees. This helps to reduce cerebral blood pressure by promoting venous return and decreasing intracranial pressure. Assess for bladder distension or bowel impaction. These are the most common triggers for autonomic dysreflexia due to noxious stimuli below the level of the spinal cord injury. Check the Foley catheter for kinks or obstruction. A full bladder can trigger autonomic dysreflexia by causing increased pressure and stimulation of the sympathetic nervous system. Administer hydralazine IV per physician's order. This medication is a direct-acting peripheral arterial vasodilator that will help lower the blood pressure quickly. Monitor blood pressure every 5 minutes after administering hydralazine. Frequent monitoring is essential to assess the effectiveness of the intervention and prevent hypotension, which can occur rapidly with hydralazine administration.
