The nurse is caring for a 6-month-old infant diagnosed with Tetralogy of Fallot who is experiencing a hypercyanotic spell (TET spell). Which of the following nursing interventions are appropriate? Select all that apply.

✓ Clinical Rationales

Placing the infant in a knee-chest position helps to increase systemic vascular resistance, reducing the right-to-left shunting of blood and improving pulmonary blood flow. This position helps to alleviate cyanosis and improve oxygen saturation.,Administering morphine sulfate intravenously helps to decrease pulmonary vascular resistance and reduce the infant's anxiety and agitation. This can break the cycle of hyperventilation and further desaturation.,Administering 100% oxygen via face mask increases the partial pressure of oxygen in the alveoli, which can help to improve oxygen saturation in the blood. Although oxygen alone may not completely resolve the cyanosis due to the underlying cardiac defect, it is an important supportive measure.,Remaining calm and reassuring the infant and family helps to reduce anxiety and agitation, which can exacerbate the hypercyanotic spell. A calm environment can help to decrease the infant's metabolic demands and improve oxygen utilization.,Monitoring the infant's oxygen saturation and vital signs continuously allows for prompt assessment of the effectiveness of interventions and early detection of any deterioration in the infant's condition. Continuous monitoring is essential for guiding further management.
