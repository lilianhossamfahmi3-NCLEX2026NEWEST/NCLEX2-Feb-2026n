✓ Clinical Rationales

Elevating the head of the bed to at least 30 degrees reduces the risk of aspiration and subsequent pneumonia. Performing frequent oral care with chlorhexidine helps to reduce the bacterial load in the oral cavity, minimizing the risk of bacteria entering the lungs. Ensuring proper hand hygiene before and after any patient contact is crucial to prevent the spread of infection. Implementing a sedation vacation daily allows for assessment of the patient's readiness to wean from the ventilator, reducing the duration of mechanical ventilation and thus the risk of VAP. Continuous subglottic suctioning removes secretions that accumulate above the endotracheal tube cuff, preventing them from being aspirated into the lungs.
