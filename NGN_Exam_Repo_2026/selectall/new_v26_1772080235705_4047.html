✓ Clinical Rationales

An elevated BNP (Brain Natriuretic Peptide) level is a strong indicator of heart failure. BNP is released by the ventricles in response to volume expansion and pressure overload, making it a useful marker for diagnosing and assessing the severity of heart failure. The failing heart releases BNP in response to ventricular stretch, making it a direct marker of heart failure exacerbation. Elevated BNP levels often correlate with the severity of heart failure symptoms.
