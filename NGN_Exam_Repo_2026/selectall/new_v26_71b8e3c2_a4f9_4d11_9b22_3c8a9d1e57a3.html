✓ Clinical Rationales

The client is exhibiting signs of acute respiratory distress syndrome (ARDS) secondary to pneumonia, characterized by refractory hypoxemia (PaO2 58 mmHg despite 60% FiO2), tachypnea, and bilateral infiltrates on chest x-ray. The primary intervention is to secure the airway and provide mechanical ventilation to support oxygenation and ventilation. Sedation and paralytics are necessary to facilitate intubation, reduce oxygen consumption, and prevent ventilator dyssynchrony. Prone positioning is a key evidence-based therapy for moderate to severe ARDS that improves ventilation-perfusion (V/Q) matching by recruiting dependent alveoli and shifting perfusion to better-aerated lung regions.
