✓ Clinical Rationales

A potassium level of 5.8 mEq/L is concerning in a patient with heart failure, especially in the context of diuretic use (furosemide) and chronic kidney disease. Hyperkalemia can lead to life-threatening arrhythmias such as ventricular fibrillation or asystole, and should be addressed promptly with medications like calcium gluconate, insulin/dextrose, or dialysis. Diuretics can cause both hypokalemia and hyperkalemia depending on the patient's renal function and other medications.
