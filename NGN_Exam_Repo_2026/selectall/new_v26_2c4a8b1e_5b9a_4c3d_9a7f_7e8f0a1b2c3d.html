✓ Clinical Rationales

Initiating continuous cardiac monitoring is vital due to the risk of dysrhythmias associated with electrolyte imbalances, especially potassium. Potassium levels fluctuate rapidly during DKA treatment. Administering intravenous fluids, typically normal saline, is crucial to correct dehydration and restore intravascular volume. Monitoring blood glucose levels hourly allows for timely adjustments to the insulin infusion rate, ensuring a gradual and safe reduction in blood glucose. Administering intravenous insulin as a continuous infusion is the cornerstone of DKA treatment, facilitating the transport of glucose into cells and correcting metabolic acidosis. Monitoring potassium levels frequently is essential because insulin administration shifts potassium intracellularly, potentially leading to hypokalemia.
