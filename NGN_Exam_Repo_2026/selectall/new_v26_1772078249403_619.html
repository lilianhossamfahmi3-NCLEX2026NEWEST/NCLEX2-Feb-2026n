✓ Clinical Rationales

Ibuprofen is a nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID) that can cause further damage to the kidneys, especially in patients with pre-existing renal impairment. NSAIDs inhibit prostaglandin synthesis, leading to afferent arteriolar vasoconstriction and decreased renal blood flow. This can precipitate acute kidney injury (AKI) in susceptible individuals. Its use should be avoided or significantly limited in patients with CKD.
