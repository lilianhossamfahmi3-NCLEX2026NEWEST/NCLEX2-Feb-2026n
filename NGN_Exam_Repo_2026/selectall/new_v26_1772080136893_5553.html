✓ Clinical Rationales

A respiratory rate of 60 and an oxygen saturation of 85% in a 4-year-old child with bronchiolitis indicate severe respiratory distress and hypoxemia. Immediate intervention is required to improve oxygenation and ventilation, such as increasing oxygen delivery via nasal cannula or face mask, and potentially escalating care to continuous positive airway pressure (CPAP) or intubation if the child's condition does not improve. The normal respiratory rate for a 4-year-old is 20-30 breaths per minute, and normal oxygen saturation is typically above 95%.
