✓ Clinical Rationales

The patient's hypotension (BP 90/60 mmHg) in the context of acute myocardial infarction is the most concerning finding. It suggests cardiogenic shock, a life-threatening complication that requires immediate intervention to improve cardiac output and tissue perfusion. The elevated heart rate is likely compensatory for the low blood pressure. Hypotension reduces coronary artery perfusion, exacerbating myocardial ischemia and potentially leading to further cardiac damage. The body attempts to compensate with tachycardia, but this increases myocardial oxygen demand.
