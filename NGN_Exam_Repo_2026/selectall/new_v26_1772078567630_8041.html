✓ Clinical Rationales

Hypotension, despite fluid resuscitation, along with other signs of infection and organ dysfunction (altered mental status, elevated WBC), is a key indicator of sepsis. Sepsis is defined as life-threatening organ dysfunction caused by a dysregulated host response to infection. Hypotension refractory to initial fluid bolus suggests significant vasodilation and impaired perfusion, a hallmark of septic shock. The body's response to infection leads to systemic inflammation, causing vasodilation and decreased blood pressure.
