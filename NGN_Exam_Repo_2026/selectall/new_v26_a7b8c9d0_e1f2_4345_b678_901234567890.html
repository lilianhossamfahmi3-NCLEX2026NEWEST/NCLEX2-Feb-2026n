✓ Clinical Rationales

Elevating the head of the bed to 30-45 degrees reduces the risk of aspiration, a major risk factor for VAP. Oral care with chlorhexidine reduces oral bacterial colonization, decreasing the risk of lower respiratory tract infection. Suctioning removes accumulated secretions, preventing bacterial pooling and colonization. Hand hygiene prevents the transmission of pathogens. Maintaining appropriate endotracheal tube cuff pressure (20-30 cm H2O) prevents leakage of contaminated secretions into the lower airway. Clinical Pearl: VAP significantly increases morbidity and mortality in mechanically ventilated patients.
