✓ Clinical Rationales

Assessing for bladder distension is crucial. Bladder distension is a common trigger for autonomic dysreflexia and requires immediate intervention to reduce the stimulus. Catheterizing the patient, if bladder distension is present, is the next essential step. This relieves the pressure and reduces the autonomic response. Monitoring blood pressure every 5 minutes is necessary to evaluate the effectiveness of interventions and identify trends in the patient's response. Administering ordered PRN antihypertensive medication is vital to control the hypertensive crisis and prevent complications such as stroke. Checking for bowel impaction is important as bowel distension is another common trigger for autonomic dysreflexia. Addressing this cause can alleviate the symptoms.
