✓ Clinical Rationales

Elevated troponin I is the most specific and sensitive marker for myocardial damage. While other lab values might be abnormal in the context of acute chest pain, a rising troponin level indicates active myocardial infarction and requires immediate intervention. Troponin levels rise within 3-12 hours of the onset of chest pain, peak at 24-48 hours, and can remain elevated for up to 2 weeks.
