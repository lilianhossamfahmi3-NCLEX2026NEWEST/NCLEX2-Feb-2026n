✓ Clinical Rationales

The correct actions to take during a Tet spell include placing the infant in the knee-chest position to increase systemic vascular resistance (SVR), administering 100% oxygen to decrease pulmonary vascular resistance (PVR), providing a calm environment to reduce oxygen demand, and administering morphine to reduce anxiety and potentially relax the infundibular muscle. Chest physiotherapy is contraindicated as it increases agitation and oxygen demand. Chest compressions are only indicated in the absence of a pulse.
