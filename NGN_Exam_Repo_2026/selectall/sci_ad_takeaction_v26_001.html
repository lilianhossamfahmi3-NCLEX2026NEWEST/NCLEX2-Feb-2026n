✓ Clinical Rationales

The client is experiencing autonomic dysreflexia (AD), a life-threatening condition triggered by a noxious stimulus below the level of a T6 or higher spinal cord injury. The pathophysiology involves an imbalanced reflex sympathetic discharge, leading to severe hypertension and bradycardia. The priority actions are to remove the stimulus and manage the hypertension. This includes assessing for and relieving bladder distention (the most common cause), checking for and removing bowel impaction (the second most common cause), administering ordered rapid-acting antihypertensives, and continuously monitoring the blood pressure to evaluate the response to interventions.
