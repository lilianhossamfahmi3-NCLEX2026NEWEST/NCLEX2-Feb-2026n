✓ Clinical Rationales

The Parkland formula (4 mL x kg x % TBSA) guides fluid resuscitation in burn patients. Half of the calculated volume is administered in the first 8 hours. For this patient: 4 mL x 70 kg x 40% = 11200 mL. Half of this (5600 mL) over 8 hours equals 700 mL/hr. Hourly urine output monitoring is essential to assess fluid status and guide further fluid administration. Elevating the burned extremity helps reduce edema formation.
