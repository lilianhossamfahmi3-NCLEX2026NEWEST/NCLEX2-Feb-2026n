✓ Clinical Rationales

The client's presentation of persistent hypotension (MAP < 65 mmHg), tachycardia, tachypnea, altered mental status, and elevated lactate indicates septic shock. The Surviving Sepsis Campaign guidelines prioritize rapid intervention. This includes aggressive fluid resuscitation (30 mL/kg crystalloid) to restore intravascular volume, obtaining blood cultures to guide antimicrobial therapy, initiating vasopressors (norepinephrine) if hypotension persists after fluids, and placing an indwelling catheter for precise hourly urine output monitoring to assess end-organ perfusion.
