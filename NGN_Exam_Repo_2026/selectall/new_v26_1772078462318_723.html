✓ Clinical Rationales

Assessing fetal heart rate (FHR) is the priority. After rupture of membranes, there is a risk of umbilical cord prolapse, which can compromise fetal oxygenation. Immediate assessment of FHR is essential to identify any signs of distress. Continuous FHR monitoring helps to detect early signs of fetal distress, allowing for timely interventions such as repositioning the mother, administering oxygen, or preparing for an emergency delivery.
