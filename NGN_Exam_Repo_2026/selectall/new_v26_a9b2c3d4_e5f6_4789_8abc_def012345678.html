✓ Clinical Rationales

Regularly assessing breath sounds is crucial to detect changes such as diminished breath sounds, which could indicate pneumothorax, atelectasis, or tube displacement. Monitoring ventilator settings helps ensure they are appropriate for the patient's needs and adjusted based on the patient's respiratory status and ABGs. Maintaining proper cuff inflation is important to prevent air leaks and aspiration. Performing oral care regularly helps prevent ventilator-associated pneumonia (VAP). Elevating the head of the bed to at least 30 degrees helps prevent aspiration and VAP.
