✓ Clinical Rationales

Assessing the high pressure alarm setting is crucial because it alerts to possible airway obstruction, such as mucus plugging or kinked tubing, which can cause respiratory distress. Evaluating the tidal volume setting is important as inadequate tidal volume can lead to hypoventilation and respiratory distress. Checking the FiO2 setting is essential to ensure adequate oxygenation and rule out hypoxemia as a cause of distress. Changes in oxygen requirements should be addressed immediately to prevent further decompensation.
