✓ Clinical Rationales

Clinical rationale: Overdamping in an arterial line system leads to inaccurate, falsely low systolic blood pressure readings. This occurs when the system's ability to transmit the arterial pressure wave is compromised, often due to obstructions or energy absorption within the system. The correct options reflect common causes of overdamping: air bubbles in the tubing, a kinked catheter, and a blood clot at the catheter tip. These factors impede the accurate transmission of the pressure wave, resulting in a blunted waveform and an underestimation of the true systolic pressure. Recognizing and addressing these issues is crucial for accurate hemodynamic monitoring and appropriate patient management.
