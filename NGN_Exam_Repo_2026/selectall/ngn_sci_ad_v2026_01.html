The client is experiencing autonomic dysreflexia (AD), a life-threatening condition triggered by a noxious stimulus below the level of a T6 or higher spinal cord injury. Uncontrolled sympathetic nervous system discharge causes severe vasoconstriction below the injury, leading to extreme hypertension. A compensatory parasympathetic response above the injury causes bradycardia, flushing, and diaphoresis. Immediate interventions must aim to lower the blood pressure and remove the stimulus.
