✓ Clinical Rationales

Tumor Lysis Syndrome (TLS) is a metabolic emergency characterized by hyperuricemia, hyperkalemia, hyperphosphatemia, and hypocalcemia. These electrolyte imbalances result from the rapid lysis of tumor cells, releasing intracellular contents into the bloodstream. Option A (Hyperkalemia), Option B (Hyperuricemia), Option C (Hyperphosphatemia), and Option D (Hypocalcemia) are all direct consequences of this cellular breakdown. Option F (Decreased urine output with cloudy appearance) indicates acute kidney injury (AKI), a common complication of TLS caused by the precipitation of uric acid and calcium phosphate crystals in the renal tubules. Option E (Drop in WBC) indicates the chemotherapy is working, but is not a direct indicator of TLS.
