✓ Clinical Rationales

Administer furosemide 40mg IV push: The patient exhibits signs of fluid overload (crackles, elevated BP, low urine output), making a loop diuretic essential to reduce preload and pulmonary congestion. IV administration ensures rapid onset of action. Initiate oxygen therapy via non-rebreather mask: The patient's SpO2 is 88% on room air, indicating hypoxemia requiring immediate intervention with a high-flow oxygen delivery system to improve oxygenation. Continuous cardiac monitoring: Given the history of MI and current ADHF, continuous monitoring is vital to detect arrhythmias or ST-segment changes, which can occur due to electrolyte imbalances or myocardial ischemia. Strict I&O monitoring: Accurately monitoring fluid balance is crucial to assess the effectiveness of diuretic therapy and guide further interventions. This helps prevent over-diuresis or persistent fluid overload. Elevate head of bed to high Fowler's position: This position promotes lung expansion and reduces venous return, easing the patient's work of breathing and improving oxygenation. It decreases preload and pulmonary congestion.
