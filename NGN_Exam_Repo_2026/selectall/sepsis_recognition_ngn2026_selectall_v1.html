The correct answers are A, B, C, and F. These clients exhibit cues most concerning for the development of sepsis. Sepsis is a life-threatening condition that arises when the body's response to an infection injures its own tissues and organs. It is characterized by signs of systemic inflammation and organ dysfunction. New onset confusion (A), tachypnea (B), hypotension (C), and tachycardia (F) are all concerning signs of sepsis. A low-grade fever (D) and leukocytosis (E) are less specific and may be present in other conditions.
