✓ Clinical Rationales

In patients with COPD, the respiratory drive is often stimulated by hypoxemia rather than hypercapnia. Administering excessive oxygen can suppress the hypoxic drive, leading to decreased respiratory rate and increased PaCO2 levels, resulting in respiratory acidosis. An oxygen saturation of 98% is above the target range (88-92% for most COPD patients) and the ABG shows a PaO2 of 95 mmHg and SaO2 of 98%, indicating that the oxygen flow rate should be reduced to avoid suppressing the respiratory drive and causing CO2 retention.
