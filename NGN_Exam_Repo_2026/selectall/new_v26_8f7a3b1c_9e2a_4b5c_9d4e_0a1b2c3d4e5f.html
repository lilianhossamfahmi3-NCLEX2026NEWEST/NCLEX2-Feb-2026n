✓ Clinical Rationales

Autonomic dysreflexia (AD) is a life-threatening emergency in clients with spinal cord injuries at T6 or above. A noxious stimulus below the injury level (e.g., bowel impaction) triggers an unopposed sympathetic response, causing intense vasoconstriction and severe hypertension. The brain detects this and attempts a parasympathetic response via the vagus nerve, but the signal is blocked at the injury site. This results in bradycardia and compensatory vasodilation *above* the injury level, causing the classic signs of a severe headache, flushing, and diaphoresis. The extreme hypertension can also lead to blurred vision, while the vasoconstriction below the injury causes cool skin and piloerection.
