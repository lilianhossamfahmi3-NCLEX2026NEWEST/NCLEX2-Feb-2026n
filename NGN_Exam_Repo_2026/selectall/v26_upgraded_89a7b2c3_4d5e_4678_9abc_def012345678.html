✓ Clinical Rationales

Correct interventions for managing increased ICP focus on promoting venous outflow, preventing further increases in pressure, and minimizing metabolic demand. Elevating the head of the bed to 30 degrees and maintaining neutral head alignment use gravity to facilitate venous drainage from the cranium. Administering stool softeners prevents the Valsalva maneuver during defecation, which sharply increases intrathoracic and intracranial pressure. Frequent neurological assessments are critical for early detection of deterioration. A low-stimulus environment reduces noxious stimuli that can trigger ICP spikes.
