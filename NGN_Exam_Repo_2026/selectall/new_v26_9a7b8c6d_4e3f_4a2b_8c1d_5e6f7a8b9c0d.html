✓ Clinical Rationales

Administering a fluid bolus is crucial to improve blood pressure and tissue perfusion in septic shock. Monitoring urine output hourly helps assess renal perfusion and response to fluid resuscitation. Obtaining serial lactate levels monitors the effectiveness of interventions in reducing anaerobic metabolism, which is elevated in sepsis due to tissue hypoperfusion. Applying cool packs can help reduce fever and metabolic demand. Maintaining contact precautions helps prevent the spread of MRSA to other patients and healthcare workers. Given the risk of aspiration pneumonia, airborne precautions should be considered until ruled out.
