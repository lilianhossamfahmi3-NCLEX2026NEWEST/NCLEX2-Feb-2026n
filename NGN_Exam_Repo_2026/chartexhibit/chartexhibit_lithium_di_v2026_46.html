A 52-year-old client with a history of long-term lithium use for bipolar disorder presents with extreme polyuria. Based on the documentation in the Vasopressin Challenge & Serum Log, what is the most likely diagnosis?

Item type [chartExhibit] rendering in simplified mode.

✓ Clinical Rationales

The client has nephrogenic diabetes insipidus (a1). The vasopressin challenge is the definitive test. In central DI, administering vasopressin (ADH) resolves the issue because the brain was not producing it. However, in nephrogenic DI (often caused by lithium-induced damage to the renal tubules), the kidneys are resistant to ADH. Therefore, administering external vasopressin does not concentrate the urine (specific gravity remains < 1.005). The history of 15 years of lithium use is the primary risk factor.
