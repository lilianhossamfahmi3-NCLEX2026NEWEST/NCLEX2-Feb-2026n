A client with Diabetic Ketoacidosis (DKA) has been on an IV insulin drip for 12 hours. Based on the documentation provided, which finding indicates that the nurse can transition the client to subcutaneous insulin?

Item type [chartExhibit] rendering in simplified mode.

✓ Clinical Rationales

The most definitive indicator of DKA resolution and readiness for SQ insulin transition is the closure of the anion gap (a1). An anion gap of 10 mEq/L (Normal is < 12) indicates that the accumulation of ketoacids has ceased and the metabolic acidosis has resolved. While glucose (a2) often drops quickly, the underlying acidosis (indicated by the anion gap) takes longer to resolve. Per the provider orders, the client also meets the glucose, bicarbonate, and oral intake criteria.
