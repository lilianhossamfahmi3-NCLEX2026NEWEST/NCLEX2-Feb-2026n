A client with diabetes insipidus (DI) is receiving intranasal desmopressin (DDAVP). Based on the trends in the Serial Osmolality & Weight Surveillance Log, which finding indicates the medication is achieving its therapeutic goal?

Item type [chartExhibit] rendering in simplified mode.

✓ Clinical Rationales

The therapeutic goal of desmopressin (a synthetic ADH) in DI is to increase water reabsorption in the kidneys, which results in more concentrated urine. An increase in urine specific gravity (a1) (from very dilute 1.002 to a normal 1.018) and a corresponding decrease in urine volume (from 800 to 120 mL/hr) are the most objective indicators of drug efficacy.
