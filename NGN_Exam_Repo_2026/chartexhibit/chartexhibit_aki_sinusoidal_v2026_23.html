A client with acute kidney injury (AKI) has a serum potassium level of 8.2 mEq/L. Based on the documentation in the Nephrology Emergency & EKG Strip Log, which medication should the nurse prioritize administering first?

Item type [chartExhibit] rendering in simplified mode.

✓ Clinical Rationales

The 'Sinusoidal' or Sine-wave EKG pattern indicates a critical stage of hyperkalemia, potentially leading to cardiac arrest (asystole or ventricular fibrillation). When the heart is unstable, the priority is to administer Calcium Gluconate. This medication does NOT lower potassium levels; instead, it antagonizes the effect of potassium on the cardiac cell membrane, stabilizing the heart and preventing a lethal rhythm. While Insulin and Albuterol shift potassium into cells, they take 15-30 minutes to take effect, which is too long in this emergency situation.
