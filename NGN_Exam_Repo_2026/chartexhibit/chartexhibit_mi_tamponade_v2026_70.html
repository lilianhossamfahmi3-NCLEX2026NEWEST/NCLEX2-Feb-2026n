The client is experiencing **cardiac tamponade (a1)**, likely caused by a wire perforation during the PCI. The hallmark cues follow **Beck's Triad**: 1. **Hypotension with narrow pulse pressure** (82/74), 2. **Distended jugular veins** (JVD), and 3. **Muffled/Distant heart sounds**. Unlike shock from a femoral bleed (a3), which would show *flat* neck veins, tamponade keeps the blood backed up in the veins because the heart is being crushed by fluid in the pericardium. Clear lungs help rule out cardiogenic shock with pulmonary edema.
