✓ Clinical Rationales

The most definitive ECG indicator of a Left Main Coronary Artery (LMCA) occlusion, often called the 'Widowmaker,' is ST-segment elevation in lead aVR accompanied by widespread/diffuse ST-segment depression in almost all other leads. Lead aVR 'looks down' into the heart from the right; ST-elevation here reflects global injury to the left ventricle because the main 'trunk' of the artery is blocked. This pattern has an extremely high mortality rate and requires immediate surgical or mechanical intervention.
