The nurse is reviewing the admission data for a client rescued from a house fire. Based on the findings in the client's chart, what is the estimated Total Body Surface Area (TBSA) percentage of the burn using the Rule of Nines?

Item type [chartExhibit] rendering in simplified mode.

✓ Clinical Rationales

The correct calculation using the Rule of Nines is 36%. The anterior trunk is 18%, the entire left arm is 9%, and the anterior aspect of the right leg is 9%. The sum is 18 + 9 + 9 = 36%. Pathophysiologically, this TBSA value is critical for calculating fluid resuscitation needs. Major burns trigger a systemic inflammatory response, causing massive capillary leakage and fluid shifts from the intravascular to the interstitial space. This leads to hypovolemic shock, as evidenced by the client's tachycardia and hypotension. Accurate TBSA calculation is the first step in using the Parkland formula to restore intravascular volume and maintain vital organ perfusion.
