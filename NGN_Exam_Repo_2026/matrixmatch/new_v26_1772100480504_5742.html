✓ Clinical Rationales

Administering oxygen to maintain SpO2 between 88-92% is appropriate for COPD patients to avoid suppressing the hypoxic drive, which is their primary stimulus for breathing. Encouraging pursed-lip breathing helps to prolong exhalation, prevent air trapping, and reduce dyspnea by creating back pressure in the airways. Positioning the client in high Fowler's promotes optimal lung expansion, facilitating improved gas exchange. Frequent monitoring of respiratory status allows for early detection of changes and timely intervention to prevent further deterioration. clinicalPearl: COPD patients retain CO2, and their bodies adapt to high CO2 levels. Therefore, their primary drive to breathe becomes hypoxia, not hypercapnia.
