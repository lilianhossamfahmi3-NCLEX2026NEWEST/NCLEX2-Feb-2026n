✓ Clinical Rationales

In ARDS, PEEP (r1) is used specifically to recruit alveoli that have collapsed due to fluid and surfactant loss. Prone positioning (r2) shifts the weight of the heart and abdominal contents away from the posterior lung, improving ventilation-perfusion matching. Low tidal volume (r3) is the gold standard for lung protection to prevent over-stretching of the delicate alveoli (barotrauma). A conservative fluid strategy (r4) helps minimize the alveolar-capillary leak, which causes the characteristic pulmonary edema of ARDS.
