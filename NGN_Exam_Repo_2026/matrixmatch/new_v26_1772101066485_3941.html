✓ Clinical Rationales

Maintaining the head of the bed at 30-45 degrees promotes venous drainage from the brain, reducing intracranial pressure (ICP). Frequent neurological assessments (e.g., pupillary response, motor strength, level of consciousness) are crucial to detect any changes in the client's condition, indicating increased ICP or neurological deficits. Monitoring fluid balance helps prevent fluid overload, which can exacerbate cerebral edema and increase ICP. Administering prescribed pain medication ensures client comfort and reduces stress, which can elevate ICP. Implementing seizure precautions is important as craniotomy increases the risk of seizures due to brain tissue manipulation.
