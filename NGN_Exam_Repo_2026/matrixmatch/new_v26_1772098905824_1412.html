✓ Clinical Rationales

Right-sided heart failure leads to systemic fluid retention, causing edema and jugular venous distention. Left-sided heart failure leads to pulmonary congestion, causing crackles and shortness of breath. Therefore, crackles, shortness of breath indicate left-sided heart failure, while jugular venous distention and edema indicate right-sided heart failure. Hypertension and normal breath sounds are not directly indicative of either condition.
