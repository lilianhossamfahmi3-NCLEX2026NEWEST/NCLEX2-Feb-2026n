✓ Clinical Rationales

Findings such as S3 gallop, elevated BNP, cardiomegaly, pulmonary edema, crackles, and lower extremity edema are indicative of both HFrEF and HFpEF. The presence of JVD is also supportive of both types of heart failure. These findings reflect the body's response to decreased cardiac output and fluid overload. Elevated BNP indicates cardiac strain, while pulmonary and peripheral edema result from increased hydrostatic pressure. An S3 gallop suggests increased ventricular filling pressure. Cardiomegaly is a compensatory mechanism. Crackles indicate fluid in the alveoli. However, to differentiate, one needs to consider the patient's ejection fraction (not provided, implying a separate assessment would be needed). Without the ejection fraction, these are general signs of heart failure.
