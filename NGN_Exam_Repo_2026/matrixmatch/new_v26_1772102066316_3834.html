✓ Clinical Rationales

The elevated TSH (Thyroid Stimulating Hormone) and low Free T4 (Thyroxine) levels indicate that the patient's hypothyroidism is not adequately controlled with the current levothyroxine dosage. TSH is high because the pituitary gland is attempting to stimulate the thyroid to produce more T4. Free T4 is the active form of the hormone. This requires immediate action to adjust the medication dosage to prevent further complications such as myxedema coma, a life-threatening condition characterized by severe metabolic slowing. The patient's symptoms (fatigue, weight gain, constipation) are consistent with hypothyroidism but do not require immediate escalation as long as the thyroid hormone levels are addressed. The goal is to restore euthyroidism (normal thyroid function).
