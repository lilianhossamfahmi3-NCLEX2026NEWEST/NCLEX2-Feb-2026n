The nurse is caring for a patient with newly diagnosed hypothyroidism. Which of the following findings require immediate intervention?

Item type [matrixMatch] rendering in simplified mode.

✓ Clinical Rationales

Significant bradycardia (HR < 60) in a hypothyroid patient can indicate severe disease and increased risk of cardiac complications such as arrhythmias or heart failure. This requires immediate intervention, possibly including holding the levothyroxine dose and notifying the physician. The decreased thyroid hormone levels affect the sinoatrial node, reducing its firing rate and leading to bradycardia. Severe bradycardia compromises cardiac output, potentially causing hypotension and inadequate tissue perfusion. Prompt intervention is crucial to prevent life-threatening complications.
