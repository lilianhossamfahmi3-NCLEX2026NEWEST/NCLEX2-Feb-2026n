✓ Clinical Rationales

Right-sided heart failure leads to systemic fluid retention, causing edema and jugular venous distention due to the heart's inability to effectively pump blood to the lungs, leading to increased pressure in the systemic venous system. Left-sided heart failure leads to pulmonary congestion, causing crackles and shortness of breath because the left ventricle cannot pump blood efficiently to the body, leading to a backup of blood into the pulmonary circulation. Elevated BNP levels are indicative of heart failure, as the heart releases BNP in response to ventricular stretching.
