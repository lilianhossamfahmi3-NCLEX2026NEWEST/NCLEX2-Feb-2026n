✓ Clinical Rationales

Elevated blood pressure and proteinuria are key indicators of preeclampsia. Elevated fasting blood glucose is a key indicator of gestational diabetes. Thrombocytopenia can be associated with preeclampsia. Preeclampsia is characterized by hypertension and proteinuria (or other signs of end-organ damage) after 20 weeks of gestation. The elevated blood pressure damages the endothelial lining of blood vessels, leading to increased vascular permeability and proteinuria. Thrombocytopenia results from platelet activation and consumption as part of the disease process. Gestational diabetes is diagnosed when glucose intolerance is first recognized during pregnancy, often due to insulin resistance caused by placental hormones. A fasting blood glucose level of 145 mg/dL exceeds the normal range for pregnant women, indicating impaired glucose metabolism.
