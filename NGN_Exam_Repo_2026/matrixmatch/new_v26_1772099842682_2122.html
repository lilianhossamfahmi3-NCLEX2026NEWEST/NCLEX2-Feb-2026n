✓ Clinical Rationales

Administering oxygen to maintain SpO2 between 88-92% is appropriate for COPD patients to avoid suppressing the hypoxic drive, which is their primary stimulus for breathing. Encouraging pursed-lip breathing helps to prolong exhalation, prevent air trapping, and reduce dyspnea by creating back pressure in the airways. Positioning the client in high Fowler's promotes optimal lung expansion and diaphragmatic descent, improving ventilation. Administering bronchodilators helps to open airways and improve airflow, reducing airway resistance and work of breathing.
