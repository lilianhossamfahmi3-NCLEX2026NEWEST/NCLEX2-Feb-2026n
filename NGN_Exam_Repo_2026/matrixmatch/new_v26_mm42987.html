✓ Clinical Rationales

The patient's aPTT is significantly elevated (92 seconds, normal 25-35 seconds), indicating excessive anticoagulation and an increased risk of bleeding. Reducing or holding the heparin infusion is crucial to prevent further complications. Protamine sulfate is the antidote for heparin and should be readily available. Monitoring for signs of bleeding (gums, urine, stool) is essential. Repeating the aPTT in one hour is appropriate to assess the effect of the dose reduction or hold.
