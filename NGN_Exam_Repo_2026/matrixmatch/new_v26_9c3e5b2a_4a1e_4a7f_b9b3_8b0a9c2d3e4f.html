✓ Clinical Rationales

Administering a vasopressor (norepinephrine) is indicated to increase blood pressure in the setting of septic shock by causing vasoconstriction and increasing systemic vascular resistance. Obtaining blood cultures before antibiotics helps identify the specific organism causing the infection, guiding targeted antibiotic therapy. Initiating broad-spectrum antibiotics empirically is crucial in sepsis management to cover potential pathogens before culture results are available, improving the chances of a positive outcome. Continuous hemodynamic monitoring is vital for assessing the client's response to interventions, detecting any deterioration, and guiding further treatment. Monitoring urine output is crucial to assess kidney function and fluid balance in the context of sepsis-induced kidney injury; decreased output may indicate worsening renal perfusion.
