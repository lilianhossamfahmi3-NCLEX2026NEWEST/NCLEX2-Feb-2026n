✓ Clinical Rationales

Hepatitis A is transmitted via the fecal-oral route (e.g., contaminated food/shellfish) and has a highly effective vaccine. Hepatitis B is transmitted via blood and body fluids (e.g., needle sticks) and also has a vaccine. Hepatitis C is primarily blood-borne (e.g., IV drug use/needle sharing) but currently has NO available vaccine, although it can be treated with antiviral medications.
