✓ Clinical Rationales

Albuterol, a beta-2 adrenergic agonist, causes bronchodilation by relaxing the smooth muscles in the airways, thereby reducing airway resistance and improving airflow. Administering oxygen is crucial to counteract hypoxemia resulting from impaired gas exchange due to bronchoconstriction. Ipratropium, an anticholinergic, further aids bronchodilation by blocking muscarinic receptors in the airways, reducing mucus production and airway constriction. These interventions rapidly improve respiratory function during an acute asthma exacerbation.
