✓ Clinical Rationales

Placenta previa is characterized by painless vaginal bleeding, a soft, non-tender uterus, and a fetal heart rate that is usually within normal limits. Abruptio placentae typically presents with painful vaginal bleeding, a firm, tender uterus, and possible fetal distress. In placenta previa, the placenta implants over or near the cervical os, leading to bleeding as the cervix dilates. Abruptio placentae involves premature separation of the placenta from the uterine wall, causing pain and potential fetal compromise. The absence of abdominal pain and uterine tenderness points away from abruptio placentae.
