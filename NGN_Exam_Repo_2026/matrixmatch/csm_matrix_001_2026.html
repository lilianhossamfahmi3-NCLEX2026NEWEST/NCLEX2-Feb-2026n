✓ Clinical Rationales

The core nursing judgment in this scenario is differentiating between the chronic, expected manifestations of Cushing's syndrome and acute, life-threatening complications. Expected findings (purple striae, hypokalemia, edema) are direct results of hypercortisolism and require management but not immediate, emergent action. Unexpected findings (hyponatremia) contradict the expected pathophysiology and require investigation. Findings requiring immediate action (fever/cough, halos around lights) signal severe complications like infection due to immunosuppression or acute glaucoma, both of which can lead to irreversible harm if not addressed urgently.
