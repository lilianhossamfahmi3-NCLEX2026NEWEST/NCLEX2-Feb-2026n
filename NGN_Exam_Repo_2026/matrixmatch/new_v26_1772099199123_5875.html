✓ Clinical Rationales

Maintaining a neutral head position promotes venous drainage from the brain and reduces intracranial pressure. Monitoring neurological status frequently allows for early detection of changes that could indicate complications such as increased ICP or hematoma formation. Administering prescribed pain medication helps manage pain and prevent increases in intracranial pressure due to pain-related stress. Monitoring fluid balance helps prevent fluid overload, which can increase intracranial pressure. Implementing seizure precautions is important because craniotomy patients are at increased risk for seizures due to brain tissue manipulation and potential irritation.
