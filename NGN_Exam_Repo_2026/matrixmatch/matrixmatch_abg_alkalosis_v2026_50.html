Respiratory alkalosis (c1) occurs during hyperventilation, where CO2 is 'blown off' (low PaCO2/r1); clinical signs include tingling and spasms due to decreased ionized calcium (r3). Metabolic alkalosis (c2) occurs when acid is lost (e.g., NG suctioning), leading to high HCO3 levels (r2); it is often accompanied by low potassium and low chloride (r4).
