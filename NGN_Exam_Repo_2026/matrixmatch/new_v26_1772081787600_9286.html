✓ Clinical Rationales

Sepsis is a life-threatening condition caused by the body's overwhelming response to an infection. Key findings supporting sepsis include fever (Temperature > 100.4°F or 38°C), tachycardia (HR > 100 bpm), tachypnea (RR > 22 breaths/min), hypotension (SBP < 90 mmHg), elevated WBC count (>12,000/μL), elevated lactate (>2 mmol/L), decreased SpO2 (<95%) and altered mental status. Procalcitonin is a marker specific for bacterial infections and is often elevated in sepsis.
