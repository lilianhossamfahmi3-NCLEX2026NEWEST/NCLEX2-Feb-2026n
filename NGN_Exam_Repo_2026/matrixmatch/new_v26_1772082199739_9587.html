✓ Clinical Rationales

The presence of an S3 heart sound, elevated BNP, pulmonary congestion on chest X-ray, lower extremity edema, and uncontrolled hypertension all support a diagnosis of HFpEF in this patient. HFpEF is characterized by impaired ventricular relaxation and diastolic dysfunction, leading to increased filling pressures and subsequent symptoms of heart failure despite a preserved ejection fraction. The elevated BNP (Brain Natriuretic Peptide) indicates increased cardiac workload and ventricular stretch, common in heart failure. Pulmonary congestion and edema result from increased pulmonary venous pressure due to the heart's inability to effectively pump blood. Uncontrolled hypertension contributes to left ventricular hypertrophy and diastolic dysfunction, key features of HFpEF.
