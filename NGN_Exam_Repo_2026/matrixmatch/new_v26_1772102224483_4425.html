✓ Clinical Rationales

Hypotension (Systolic BP <90 mmHg) and decreased urine output (20 mL/hr) are critical indicators of organ hypoperfusion in sepsis, suggesting the patient is progressing towards septic shock. Immediate intervention is required to improve blood pressure and tissue perfusion to prevent further organ damage and potential mortality. Septic shock is characterized by profound circulatory, cellular, and metabolic abnormalities associated with a greater risk of mortality than sepsis alone. The body's response to infection leads to widespread inflammation, vasodilation, and increased capillary permeability, resulting in hypotension and decreased tissue perfusion. The kidneys, being highly sensitive to changes in blood pressure and perfusion, respond by reducing urine output.
