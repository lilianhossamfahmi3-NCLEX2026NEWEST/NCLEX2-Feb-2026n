The nurse is caring for a client who is one day post-operative following a craniotomy. Which nursing interventions are most important to include in the client's plan of care?

✓ Clinical Rationales

Maintaining a neutral head position promotes venous drainage from the brain and reduces intracranial pressure (ICP). Elevating the head of the bed 30-45 degrees is also helpful, but neutral positioning prevents neck flexion which can impede venous return. Frequent neurological assessments are essential for early detection of changes indicating complications such as increased ICP or bleeding. Administering prescribed pain medication helps manage pain, preventing increases in ICP due to pain-related stress. Monitoring fluid balance helps prevent fluid overload or dehydration, both of which can affect ICP. Implementing seizure precautions is important because craniotomy increases the risk of seizures due to brain tissue manipulation. clinicalPearl: Subtle changes in neurological status (e.g., restlessness, subtle changes in speech) can be early indicators of increased ICP. Prompt intervention is crucial. mnemonic: NEURO: Neutral head, Evaluate neuro status, Use pain meds, Regulate fluids, Observe for seizures.
