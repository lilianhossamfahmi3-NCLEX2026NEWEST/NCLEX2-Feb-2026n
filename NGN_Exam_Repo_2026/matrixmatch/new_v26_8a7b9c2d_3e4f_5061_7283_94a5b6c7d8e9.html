✓ Clinical Rationales

Autonomic dysreflexia is a medical emergency caused by a noxious stimulus below the level of a spinal cord injury (T6 or higher). This stimulus triggers an unopposed sympathetic reflex, causing intense vasoconstriction below the injury level and leading to severe hypertension. Baroreceptors detect this hypertension and activate a parasympathetic response (vagal stimulation), but it can only act above the injury level, causing bradycardia and vasodilation. The correct management involves a multi-pronged approach: immediately removing the cause, using gravity to lower BP, administering fast-acting vasodilators if needed, and closely monitoring the response.
