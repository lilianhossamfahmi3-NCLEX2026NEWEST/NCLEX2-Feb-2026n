✓ Clinical Rationales

Personality disorders are characterized by inflexible and enduring behavior patterns that deviate significantly from cultural expectations, causing distress or impairment. * **Borderline Personality Disorder (BPD):** Marked by emotional dysregulation, unstable relationships, distorted self-image, and impulsivity. 'Splitting' is a defense mechanism where individuals view others as all good or all bad. The fear of abandonment is a core feature. * **Antisocial Personality Disorder (ASPD):** Defined by a pervasive pattern of disregard for and violation of the rights of others. Individuals with ASPD often lack empathy, are impulsive, deceitful, and may have a history of criminal behavior. * **Narcissistic Personality Disorder (NPD):** Characterized by a grandiose sense of self-importance, a need for excessive admiration, and a lack of empathy. Individuals with NPD often believe they are special and entitled. * **Obsessive-Compulsive Personality Disorder (OCPD):** Characterized by a preoccupation with orderliness, perfectionism, and control, often at the expense of flexibility and efficiency. Unlike Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder (OCD), OCPD does not involve true obsessions and compulsions.
