✓ Clinical Rationales

The priority action is to massage the fundus. A boggy, non-midline uterus indicates uterine atony, a primary cause of postpartum hemorrhage. Massaging the fundus stimulates uterine contraction, which helps to compress the blood vessels and reduce bleeding. Addressing uterine atony is crucial to prevent further blood loss and potential complications. The uterus should be massaged until it becomes firm and midline. Continuous assessment is needed to ensure the uterus remains contracted.
