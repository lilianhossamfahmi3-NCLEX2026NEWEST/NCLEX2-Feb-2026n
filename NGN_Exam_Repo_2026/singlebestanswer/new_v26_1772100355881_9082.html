The nurse is caring for a client at 39 weeks gestation who reports decreased fetal movement over the past 24 hours. Which action should the nurse prioritize?

Item type [singleBestAnswer] rendering in simplified mode.

✓ Clinical Rationales

Decreased fetal movement can indicate fetal compromise and requires immediate investigation. The nurse should prioritize assessing fetal heart rate and activity via a non-stress test (NST) to determine fetal well-being. An NST monitors the fetal heart rate in response to fetal movements. A reactive NST (normal) shows a normal baseline heart rate with accelerations, indicating adequate oxygenation and fetal well-being.
