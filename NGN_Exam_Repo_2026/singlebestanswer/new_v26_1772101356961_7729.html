✓ Clinical Rationales

A urine output of 10 mL/hr indicates inadequate kidney perfusion and potential acute kidney injury (AKI) due to sepsis-induced hypoperfusion. Immediate intervention, such as increasing intravenous fluids or adjusting vasopressor support, is needed to improve renal perfusion and prevent further kidney damage. Sepsis causes systemic vasodilation and increased capillary permeability, leading to decreased circulating volume and reduced organ perfusion. The kidneys are particularly vulnerable to this hypoperfusion, and a urine output of less than 0.5 mL/kg/hr is a critical indicator of renal dysfunction.
