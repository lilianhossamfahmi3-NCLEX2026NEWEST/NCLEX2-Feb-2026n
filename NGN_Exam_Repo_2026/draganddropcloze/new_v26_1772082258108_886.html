✓ Clinical Rationales

1. Perform a non-stress test (NST) is the correct action to directly assess fetal heart rate in response to fetal movement, providing immediate information about fetal well-being. The NST is a non-invasive method that monitors the fetal heart rate in response to fetal movements. A reactive NST (two or more accelerations of the fetal heart rate within a 20-minute period) indicates good fetal well-being. 2. Biophysical profile (BPP) provides a more comprehensive evaluation of fetal well-being, including amniotic fluid volume, fetal breathing movements, fetal tone, and gross body movements, in addition to fetal heart rate. A BPP score of 8-10 is considered normal, while a score of 6 or less may indicate fetal compromise. 3. Fetal compromise is a potential sign of decreased fetal movement. Prompt evaluation allows for timely intervention if necessary. Decreased fetal movement can be associated with placental insufficiency, fetal hypoxia, or other underlying conditions that require immediate attention.
