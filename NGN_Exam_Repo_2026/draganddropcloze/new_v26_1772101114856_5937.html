A potassium level of 3.2 mEq/L requires immediate intervention because furosemide, a loop diuretic, can cause hypokalemia. Hypokalemia can lead to cardiac arrhythmias and muscle weakness. A blood pressure of 90/60 mmHg requires immediate intervention as it indicates hypotension, which can be exacerbated by furosemide's diuretic effect, potentially leading to decreased organ perfusion.
