✓ Clinical Rationales

Sepsis is defined as life-threatening organ dysfunction caused by a dysregulated host response to infection. The body's response to an infection triggers a cascade of inflammatory mediators, leading to widespread inflammation. This systemic inflammation can cause damage to various organs, leading to organ dysfunction or failure. Early interventions, such as administering antibiotics to target the source of infection and initiating fluid resuscitation to support blood pressure and organ perfusion, are critical in managing sepsis and improving patient outcomes.
