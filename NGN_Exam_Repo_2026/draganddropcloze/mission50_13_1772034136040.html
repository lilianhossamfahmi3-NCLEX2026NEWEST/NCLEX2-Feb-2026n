✓ Clinical Rationales

Cushing's triad is a late sign of increased intracranial pressure (ICP) and indicates impending brain herniation. The triad consists of bradycardia, hypertension with widening pulse pressure, and irregular respirations. The nurse's immediate actions should be aimed at reducing ICP and supporting cerebral perfusion. Elevating the head of the bed helps to promote venous drainage from the brain, reducing ICP. Subsequently, notifying the provider is essential to facilitate further interventions such as medication administration or surgical intervention.
