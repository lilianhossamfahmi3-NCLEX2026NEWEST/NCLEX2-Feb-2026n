✓ Clinical Rationales

Elevated TSH and Decreased Free T4 are characteristic findings in hypothyroidism. The pituitary gland releases TSH to stimulate the thyroid gland to produce T4. When T4 levels are low, the pituitary gland releases more TSH in an attempt to stimulate T4 production, leading to elevated TSH levels. Free T4 is the unbound, active form of thyroxine, and its level decreases in hypothyroidism due to reduced thyroid hormone production.
