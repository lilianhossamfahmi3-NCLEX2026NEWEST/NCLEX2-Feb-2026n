✓ Clinical Rationales

The correct matching helps to localize the area of the brain affected and determine the potential underlying cause (e.g., stroke). Right-sided weakness (hemiparesis) and slurred speech (dysarthria) often indicate involvement of the left hemisphere due to the corticospinal tract decussation. Occlusion of the left middle cerebral artery (MCA) is a common cause of these symptoms, as the MCA supplies blood to the lateral aspects of the frontal, temporal, and parietal lobes, including motor and speech areas. The internal capsule contains densely packed motor fibers; a lesion here disrupts motor pathways, leading to contralateral weakness. Brainstem lesions can affect cranial nerve nuclei, leading to dysarthria and other neurological deficits.
