✓ Clinical Rationales

Initiating airborne precautions immediately is crucial to prevent the spread of TB to other patients and staff. Placing the patient in a negative pressure room and ensuring staff wear N95 respirators are essential steps. Obtaining sputum specimens confirms the diagnosis and guides treatment. Administering antitubercular medications is important but secondary to containing the infection. Providing emotional support is always valuable but less urgent than infection control measures.
