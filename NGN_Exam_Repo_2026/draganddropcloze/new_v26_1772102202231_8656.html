✓ Clinical Rationales

Uterine atony, retained placental fragments, and lacerations of the birth canal are all risk factors for postpartum hemorrhage because they interfere with the uterus's ability to contract effectively and control bleeding after delivery. Fundal massage stimulates uterine contractions to help control bleeding. Uterotonic medications, such as oxytocin, help the uterus contract. Assessing vital signs helps monitor the patient's hemodynamic status and detect early signs of hypovolemic shock.
