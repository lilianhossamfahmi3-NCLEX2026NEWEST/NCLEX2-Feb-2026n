✓ Clinical Rationales

ACE Inhibitors (like Lisinopril) block the conversion of angiotensin I to angiotensin II, reducing vasoconstriction and aldosterone secretion, thus lowering blood pressure and reducing fluid retention. Beta-blockers (like Metoprolol) block the effects of adrenaline, slowing heart rate and lowering blood pressure, decreasing cardiac workload. Loop diuretics (like Furosemide) promote sodium and water excretion in the loop of Henle, reducing fluid overload and pulmonary congestion.
