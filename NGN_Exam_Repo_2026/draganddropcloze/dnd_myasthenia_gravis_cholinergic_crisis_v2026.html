✓ Clinical Rationales

Atropine is an anticholinergic medication used to counteract the effects of excessive acetylcholine. In a cholinergic crisis, the excessive acetylcholine at the neuromuscular junction leads to bradycardia, increased secretions, and muscle weakness. Atropine will block the acetylcholine receptors, thereby increasing the heart rate and drying secretions. Severe bradycardia (HR 48 bpm) is the most critical concern as it indicates a significant compromise in cardiac function and perfusion. Untreated bradycardia can lead to further hemodynamic instability and potentially cardiac arrest.
