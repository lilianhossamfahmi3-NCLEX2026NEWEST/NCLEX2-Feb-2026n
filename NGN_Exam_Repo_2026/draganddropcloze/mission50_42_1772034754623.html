✓ Clinical Rationales

A dampened arterial waveform indicates a decrease in the dynamic response of the arterial line system, which can lead to inaccurate readings, particularly underestimation of systolic pressure and overestimation of diastolic pressure. The FIRST action is to assess the patient and system to rule out any physiological causes or kinks in the tubing. After assessing the patient and ruling out any obvious issues, the nurse should then flush the arterial line to clear any potential clots or air bubbles that may be contributing to the dampened waveform.
