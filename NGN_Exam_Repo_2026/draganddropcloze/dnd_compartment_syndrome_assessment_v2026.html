✓ Clinical Rationales

Assessing the patient's pain level and characteristics is crucial because pain out of proportion to the injury, especially pain with passive stretching of the muscles within the compartment, is an early and key indicator of compartment syndrome. Compartment pressure exceeding 30 mmHg indicates that the pressure within the muscle compartment is high enough to compromise tissue perfusion, leading to ischemia and potential necrosis. The provider needs to be notified immediately for further evaluation and potential intervention (fasciotomy).
