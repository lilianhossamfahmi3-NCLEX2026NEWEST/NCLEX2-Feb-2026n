✓ Clinical Rationales

Sepsis is defined as life-threatening organ dysfunction caused by a dysregulated host response to infection. The body's response to an infection triggers a cascade of inflammatory mediators, leading to widespread inflammation. This systemic inflammation results in damage to tissues and organs, ultimately causing organ dysfunction. Early recognition and treatment, including antibiotics and supportive care, are crucial to improving patient outcomes and preventing septic shock. clinicalPearl: Always consider sepsis in patients presenting with fever, tachycardia, and altered mental status, especially if an infection is suspected. questionTrap: Students may confuse sepsis with a localized infection or fail to recognize the systemic nature of the condition. mnemonic: SIRS - Systemic Inflammatory Response Syndrome (precursor to sepsis).
