✓ Clinical Rationales

The completed statements correctly identify key nursing interventions for an infant with Tetralogy of Fallot (TOF). Preventing agitation and crying is paramount as these activities increase oxygen demand and can trigger a hypercyanotic 'Tet spell' by worsening the right-to-left shunt. Spacing out care activities minimizes stimulation and conserves the infant's limited energy reserves. During a Tet spell, the knee-chest position is the immediate priority; it increases systemic vascular resistance (SVR), which reduces the amount of deoxygenated blood shunting into the systemic circulation and promotes pulmonary blood flow. Morphine is administered to reduce infundibular spasm and for its sedative effects, which decrease agitation and oxygen consumption. Maintaining adequate hydration is crucial to prevent hemoconcentration from compensatory polycythemia, reducing the risk of a thromboembolic event like a stroke.
