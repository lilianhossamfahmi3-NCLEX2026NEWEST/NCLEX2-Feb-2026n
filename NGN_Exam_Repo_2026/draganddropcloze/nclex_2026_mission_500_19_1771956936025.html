✓ Clinical Rationales

Concurrent administration of heparin and warfarin increases the risk of bleeding due to their synergistic anticoagulant effects. Monitoring the aPTT is crucial for heparin, while PT/INR is for warfarin, though the INR is already elevated due to warfarin. A decreasing platelet count may indicate heparin-induced thrombocytopenia (HIT), requiring immediate intervention. The therapeutic INR range for atrial fibrillation and DVT is typically 2.0-3.0. An INR above this range further elevates bleeding risk. Vitamin K is the antidote for warfarin and would be indicated for an INR above the therapeutic range, in the presence of bleeding, or if emergent surgery is needed. Protamine sulfate is the antidote for heparin.
