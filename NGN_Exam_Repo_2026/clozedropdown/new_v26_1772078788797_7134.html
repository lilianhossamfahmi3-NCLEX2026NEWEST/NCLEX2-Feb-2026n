✓ Clinical Rationales

Acute kidney injury (AKI) is characterized by a rapid decline in kidney function, often indicated by rising creatinine and decreased urine output, which aligns with the patient's presentation. The kidneys' inability to filter waste products leads to the accumulation of toxins in the blood, causing further complications if not addressed promptly. Early intervention is crucial to prevent irreversible damage and improve patient outcomes. Monitoring fluid balance and electrolyte levels is also essential in managing AKI. Addressing the underlying cause, such as sepsis, is paramount in the treatment strategy.
