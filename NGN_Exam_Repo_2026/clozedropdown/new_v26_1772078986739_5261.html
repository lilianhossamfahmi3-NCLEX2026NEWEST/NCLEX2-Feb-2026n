✓ Clinical Rationales

Acidosis: A PaCO2 of 55 mmHg indicates respiratory acidosis due to the retention of carbon dioxide. The normal range for PaCO2 is 35-45 mmHg. In COPD patients, the target range may be slightly higher, but 55 mmHg still indicates significant CO2 retention. Interventions will focus on improving ventilation to expel excess CO2 and correct the pH. clinicalPearl: PaCO2 reflects the respiratory component of acid-base balance. Hypercapnia (elevated PaCO2) typically leads to respiratory acidosis. mnemonic: ROME (Respiratory Opposite Metabolic Equal) describes the relationship between pH and PaCO2 (respiratory) and pH and HCO3 (metabolic).
