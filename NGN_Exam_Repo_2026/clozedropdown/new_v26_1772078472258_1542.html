✓ Clinical Rationales

Broad-spectrum antibiotics should be administered as soon as possible in cases of suspected bacterial meningitis, even before lumbar puncture results are available. Delaying antibiotic administration can significantly increase the risk of morbidity and mortality. Early administration targets the causative bacteria, preventing further neurological damage and systemic complications.
