A lithium level of 2.0 mEq/L represents severe toxicity. Lithium, a mood stabilizer, affects neuronal signal transduction by influencing various neurotransmitter systems, including serotonin, norepinephrine, and dopamine. Severe toxicity at this level disrupts normal cellular function, leading to neurological, cardiovascular, and renal complications. Levels in this range can cause seizures, cardiac arrhythmias, and irreversible kidney damage due to lithium's interference with sodium transport in renal tubules, hence requiring immediate intervention. Moderate to severe toxicity (1.5-2.0 mEq/L) involves pronounced gastrointestinal distress, worsening tremors, confusion, and incoordination, stemming from lithium's impact on neuronal excitability and cellular electrolyte balance.
