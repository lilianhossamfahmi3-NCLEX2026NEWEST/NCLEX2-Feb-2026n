✓ Clinical Rationales

Abdominal distension is a key sign of perforation following a barium enema reduction for intussusception. Perforation can lead to peritonitis and sepsis. Broad-spectrum antibiotics are essential in treating peritonitis resulting from perforation, preventing further systemic infection by covering a wide range of potential bacterial pathogens released into the peritoneal cavity. Antibiotics help control the infection and prevent septic shock, which is a life-threatening complication.
