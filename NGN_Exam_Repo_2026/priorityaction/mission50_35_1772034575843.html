✓ Clinical Rationales

The priority in DKA management is to address the life-threatening issues first. Initial actions should focus on fluid resuscitation to correct dehydration and electrolyte imbalances, followed by insulin administration to address hyperglycemia and ketogenesis. Potassium replacement is crucial to prevent hypokalemia as insulin drives potassium into cells. Finally, identifying and treating the underlying cause (e.g., infection) addresses the trigger for DKA. Delaying any of these steps can lead to worsening acidosis, electrolyte imbalances, and potentially fatal outcomes.
