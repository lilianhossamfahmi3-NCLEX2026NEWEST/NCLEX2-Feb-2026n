1. Administer prescribed pre-operative sedation: Reduces anxiety and oxygen consumption, preventing hypercyanotic spells. Anxious infants are more prone to these episodes, which can be life-threatening. 2. Ensure IV access is patent and secure: Crucial for rapid medication administration (e.g., vasopressors, fluids) during and after surgery, especially given the risk of hemodynamic instability. 3. Monitor oxygen saturation continuously: Early detection of desaturation allows for prompt intervention (e.g., oxygen administration, knee-chest positioning) and prevents severe hypoxemia. 4. Communicate with the surgical team regarding the recent hypercyanotic spell: Informs the team about the infant's instability and the need for potential intraoperative interventions. 5. Review pre-operative checklist and confirm NPO status: Prevents aspiration during anesthesia.
