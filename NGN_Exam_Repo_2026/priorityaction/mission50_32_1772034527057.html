✓ Clinical Rationales

Compartment syndrome is a limb-threatening condition caused by increased pressure within a confined space, leading to decreased blood flow and nerve damage. The priority is to immediately assess the 6 P's (Pain, Pallor, Pulselessness, Paresthesia, Paralysis, and Pressure) to evaluate for compartment syndrome. Notifying the surgeon promptly is crucial for potential fasciotomy to relieve the pressure. Elevating the extremity at heart level can help reduce edema but is secondary to immediate assessment. Administering more pain medication will not address the underlying cause and may mask worsening symptoms.
