✓ Clinical Rationales

Initiating an IV infusion of normal saline is the priority action in DKA management due to significant dehydration from osmotic diuresis caused by hyperglycemia. Addressing the fluid deficit will improve perfusion and facilitate the correction of electrolyte imbalances and hyperglycemia. While insulin administration and electrolyte replacement are important, fluid resuscitation should precede them to prevent rapid shifts in electrolytes and cerebral edema.
