For a 5-year-old patient with a peanut allergy experiencing acute respiratory distress, which intervention is the HIGHEST priority?

Item type [priorityAction] rendering in simplified mode.

✓ Clinical Rationales

Administer intramuscular epinephrine. Epinephrine is the first-line treatment for anaphylaxis because it rapidly reverses the life-threatening effects of the allergic reaction. It works by constricting blood vessels to raise blood pressure, relaxing smooth muscles in the lungs to improve breathing, reducing swelling, and stimulating the heart. In acute respiratory distress due to anaphylaxis, immediate epinephrine administration can be life-saving.
