Initiating or optimizing oxygen therapy is the priority in septic shock to address tissue hypoxia and improve oxygen delivery to vital organs. Septic shock leads to vasodilation and decreased tissue perfusion, causing oxygen demand to exceed supply. Immediate oxygen support can mitigate the detrimental effects of hypoxia and support cellular function, improving patient outcomes in the critical initial phase of septic shock.
