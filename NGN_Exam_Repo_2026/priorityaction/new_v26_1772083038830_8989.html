A patient with a history of poorly controlled type 1 diabetes mellitus is admitted for diabetic ketoacidosis (DKA). The patient is currently receiving an insulin drip and IV fluids. Which of the following actions is the MOST important for the nurse to take?

Item type [priorityAction] rendering in simplified mode.

✓ Clinical Rationales

Monitor the patient's serum potassium level and administer potassium replacement as prescribed. DKA causes intracellular potassium to shift extracellularly, leading to a falsely normal or elevated serum potassium level. As acidosis is corrected with insulin, potassium shifts back into the cells, potentially causing life-threatening hypokalemia. Therefore, monitoring and correcting potassium levels is crucial to prevent cardiac arrhythmias and respiratory muscle weakness.
