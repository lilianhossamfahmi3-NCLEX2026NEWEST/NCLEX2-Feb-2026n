1. Initiate IV fluid bolus (0.9% NaCl) to address dehydration and hypovolemia. Rapid volume expansion is crucial to stabilize blood pressure and improve organ perfusion. In HHS, severe hyperglycemia leads to osmotic diuresis, causing profound dehydration and electrolyte imbalances. Rapid fluid replacement helps restore circulating volume and prevent hypovolemic shock. 2. Begin continuous regular insulin infusion to gradually reduce blood glucose levels. A slow and steady decrease prevents rapid shifts in osmolality, which can cause cerebral edema. Insulin facilitates glucose uptake into cells, lowering serum glucose levels.
