✓ Clinical Rationales

The patient's potassium level of 6.8 mEq/L represents hyperkalemia and requires immediate intervention to prevent cardiac arrhythmias. The pH of 7.25 also indicates acidosis, which can be exacerbated by hyperkalemia. Addressing the hyperkalemia is paramount. Hyperkalemia can lead to fatal arrhythmias if not promptly treated. Interventions include administering calcium gluconate to stabilize cardiac membranes, insulin and glucose to shift potassium intracellularly, and potentially dialysis to remove excess potassium.
