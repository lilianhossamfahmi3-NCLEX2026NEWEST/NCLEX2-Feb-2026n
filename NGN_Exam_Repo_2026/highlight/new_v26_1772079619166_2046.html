Cyanosis, even mild, indicates significant hypoxemia and requires immediate intervention to improve oxygenation. This is a late sign of respiratory distress in children, suggesting severely compromised gas exchange. Immediate interventions include administering high-flow oxygen, assisting with ventilation if needed, and preparing for possible intubation. The presence of cyanosis signifies that the child's compensatory mechanisms are failing, and urgent action is required to prevent further deterioration.
