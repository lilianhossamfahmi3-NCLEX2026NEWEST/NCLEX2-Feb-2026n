✓ Clinical Rationales

Anaphylactic shock is a severe, life-threatening allergic reaction. Key symptoms requiring immediate intervention include airway compromise (inspiratory stridor, wheezing, facial angioedema), cardiovascular instability (hypotension), and subjective feelings of impending doom. These symptoms indicate a rapidly escalating systemic reaction requiring immediate medical attention.
