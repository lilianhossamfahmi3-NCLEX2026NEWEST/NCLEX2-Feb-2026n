✓ Clinical Rationales

The highlighted findings are indicative of a DVT. A calf circumference difference of ≥2 cm suggests unilateral edema, a key sign of DVT. Warmth and erythema in the affected extremity result from inflammation and increased blood flow due to the thrombus. Pain, especially exacerbated by dorsiflexion, arises from venous distension and inflammation irritating nerve endings. These findings, in the context of post-operative immobility, strongly suggest DVT. clinicalPearl: While Homan's sign (pain on dorsiflexion) is a classic sign, it's unreliable and not recommended due to low sensitivity and the risk of dislodging the clot. Rely on objective findings like calf circumference and warmth. Early ambulation and prophylactic anticoagulation are crucial for DVT prevention in post-operative patients.
