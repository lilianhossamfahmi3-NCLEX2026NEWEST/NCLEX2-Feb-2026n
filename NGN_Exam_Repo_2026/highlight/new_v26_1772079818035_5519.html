✓ Clinical Rationales

Elevated white blood cell count (WBC > 12,000) and hypotension (SBP < 90 mmHg) are two key criteria for the diagnosis of sepsis. Elevated lactate is also a sign of tissue hypoperfusion, often associated with sepsis. Sepsis is defined as life-threatening organ dysfunction caused by a dysregulated host response to infection. The body's response to an infection can spiral out of control, leading to widespread inflammation and damage to tissues and organs. Early recognition and treatment are crucial to improving patient outcomes.
