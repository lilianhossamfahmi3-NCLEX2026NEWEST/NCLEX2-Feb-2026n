✓ Clinical Rationales

HELLP syndrome is a severe complication of preeclampsia characterized by Hemolysis, Elevated Liver enzymes, and Low Platelets. * **Hemolysis:** The breakdown of red blood cells leads to anemia (low hemoglobin and hematocrit) and the presence of schistocytes (fragmented RBCs) on a blood smear. This process also elevates bilirubin levels as hemoglobin is broken down, and LDH is released from damaged cells. * **Elevated Liver Enzymes:** Liver damage causes the release of AST and ALT into the bloodstream, resulting in elevated levels. Right upper quadrant (RUQ) or epigastric pain occurs due to distension of the liver capsule from swelling and inflammation. * **Low Platelets:** Platelet consumption leads to thrombocytopenia (platelet count <100,000/mm³), increasing the risk of bleeding. Proteinuria, blurry vision, and headache are indicative of the underlying severe preeclampsia, which often precedes HELLP syndrome. These findings reflect endothelial dysfunction and increased vascular permeability.
