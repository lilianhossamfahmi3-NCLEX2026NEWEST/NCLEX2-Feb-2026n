✓ Clinical Rationales

Maintaining the head of the bed at 30-45 degrees promotes venous drainage from the brain, reducing ICP. Administering stool softeners prevents straining during bowel movements, which can increase ICP. Monitoring neurological status frequently allows for early detection of changes in ICP, enabling timely intervention. Spacing nursing activities reduces stimulation and prevents sudden increases in ICP. These interventions collectively aim to minimize factors that exacerbate ICP and support cerebral perfusion. Clinical Pearl: Elevating the head of the bed is a cornerstone intervention for managing increased ICP, facilitating venous outflow and reducing cerebral edema. Mnemonic: ELEVATE - Elevate head, Limit stimulation, Evaluate neuro status, Venous drainage, Avoid straining, Time activities.
