✓ Clinical Rationales

The arterial blood gas (ABG) results showing a PaCO2 of 60 mmHg indicate acute respiratory acidosis and hypoxemia, requiring immediate intervention to improve ventilation. This is the most life-threatening finding. Elevated PaCO2 indicates that the patient is not effectively eliminating carbon dioxide, leading to a buildup of acid in the blood. Hypoxemia further exacerbates the situation by reducing oxygen delivery to vital organs. Immediate interventions may include increasing oxygen delivery, assisting with ventilation (e.g., BiPAP), and administering bronchodilators to improve airflow.
