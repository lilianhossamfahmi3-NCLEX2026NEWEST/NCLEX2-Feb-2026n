✓ Clinical Rationales

In septic shock, massive vasodilation and increased capillary permeability lead to a relative hypovolemia and maldistribution of blood flow. This results in inadequate tissue perfusion and cellular hypoxia. The highlighted findings are direct consequences of this state. Altered mental status, cool/clammy skin with weak pulses, oliguria, hypotension, and elevated lactate are classic signs of end-organ damage and anaerobic metabolism due to insufficient oxygen delivery.
