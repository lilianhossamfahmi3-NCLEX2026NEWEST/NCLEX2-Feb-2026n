✓ Clinical Rationales

The highlighted findings are consistent with the pathophysiology of anorexia nervosa. A BMI of 16.2 kg/m² indicates severe underweight, reflecting chronic energy deficit. Lanugo, fine downy hair, develops as the body attempts to conserve heat due to decreased subcutaneous fat. Bradycardia (HR 42 bpm) and hypotension (88/52 mmHg) are compensatory mechanisms to reduce cardiac workload in response to decreased metabolic demands and electrolyte imbalances. Amenorrhea, the absence of menstruation, results from hypothalamic-pituitary-ovarian axis dysfunction due to malnutrition and hormonal imbalances. Hypothermia (96.8°F/36.0°C) is another manifestation of the body's attempt to conserve energy. Parotid gland enlargement can occur due to repeated vomiting in the binge-purge subtype, causing salivary gland inflammation.
