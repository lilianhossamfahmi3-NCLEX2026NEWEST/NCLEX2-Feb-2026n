✓ Clinical Rationales

The combination of a declining level of consciousness (LOC), new pupillary changes (anisocoria), worsening focal motor deficits, and the onset of Cushing's response (hypertension, bradycardia, irregular respirations) are classic and critical indicators of increased intracranial pressure (ICP). These findings suggest that cerebral edema from the stroke is causing brain tissue to swell, leading to compression of vital structures like the brainstem and cranial nerves, which requires immediate intervention to prevent herniation.
