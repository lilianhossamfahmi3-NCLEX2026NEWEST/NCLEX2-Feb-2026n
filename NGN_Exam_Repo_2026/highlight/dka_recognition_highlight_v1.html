✓ Clinical Rationales

All highlighted cues are classic indicators of DKA. Nausea and vomiting lead to dehydration and electrolyte imbalances. Lethargy indicates central nervous system (CNS) depression due to metabolic acidosis and cerebral dehydration. Warm, dry skin with poor turgor signifies severe dehydration resulting from osmotic diuresis. Fruity breath is due to acetone production from ketone metabolism, and Kussmaul respirations (deep and rapid) are the body's attempt to compensate for metabolic acidosis by blowing off CO2. Abdominal pain is common in DKA due to gastric stasis and ileus. Hyperglycemia, hypotension, and tachycardia are hallmark signs of fluid volume deficit and the body's attempt to maintain cardiac output in the setting of dehydration and acidosis. The underlying pathophysiology involves insulin deficiency, leading to unrestrained lipolysis and ketone body formation, resulting in metabolic acidosis and osmotic diuresis.
