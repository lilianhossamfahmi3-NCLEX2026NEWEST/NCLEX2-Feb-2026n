✓ Clinical Rationales

Autonomic dysreflexia is a medical emergency characterized by a sudden onset of excessively high blood pressure. It is most commonly seen in individuals with spinal cord injuries at or above the T6 level. The body's normal feedback mechanisms to regulate blood pressure are disrupted, leading to an exaggerated response to stimuli below the level of the injury. Immediate notification of the healthcare provider is crucial to prevent severe complications such as stroke, seizure, or even death.
