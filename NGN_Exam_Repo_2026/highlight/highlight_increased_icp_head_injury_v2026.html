✓ Clinical Rationales

The highlighted findings indicate increasing intracranial pressure (ICP) and require immediate notification of the healthcare provider. Restlessness and agitation, unequal pupil size (anisocoria), hypotension, bradycardia and irregular respirations, and clear nasal drainage (potential CSF leak) are all critical signs of neurological deterioration. The combination of hypotension, bradycardia and irregular respirations can be indicative of Cushing's Triad, a late sign of increased ICP.
