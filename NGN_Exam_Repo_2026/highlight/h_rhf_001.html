✓ Clinical Rationales

Right-sided heart failure (often caused by cor pulmonale in COPD) results in systemic venous congestion. Key findings include: Jugular Venous Distension (JVD) due to increased central venous pressure, hepatomegaly (enlarged, tender liver) from hepatic congestion, peripheral edema (3+ pitting edema extending to the mid-calf) due to increased hydrostatic pressure in the capillaries, and rapid weight gain/tight jewelry reflecting fluid retention. These findings indicate the heart's inability to effectively pump blood to the lungs, leading to a backup of blood in the systemic circulation.
