✓ Clinical Rationales

A blood pressure of 90/58 mmHg indicates hypotension, a critical sign of septic shock, necessitating immediate intervention to improve perfusion. Hypotension compromises oxygen delivery to vital organs, potentially leading to organ damage and failure. Immediate actions include fluid resuscitation to increase intravascular volume and, if fluids are insufficient, vasopressors to constrict blood vessels and elevate blood pressure. Monitoring the patient's response to these interventions is crucial to prevent further complications. clinicalPearl: Hypotension in sepsis is a late sign of decompensation, requiring rapid and aggressive management to prevent irreversible organ damage.
