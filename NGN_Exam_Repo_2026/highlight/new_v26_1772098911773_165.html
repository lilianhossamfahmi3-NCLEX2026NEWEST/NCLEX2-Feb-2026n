✓ Clinical Rationales

A decreased respiratory rate of 8 breaths per minute in a COPD patient receiving oxygen suggests potential oxygen-induced hypoventilation. COPD patients rely on chronic hypoxia to stimulate breathing (hypoxic drive). Excessive oxygen can suppress this drive, leading to respiratory depression and CO2 retention (hypercapnia). Immediate intervention is needed to prevent respiratory arrest. The target SpO2 for COPD patients is generally 88-92%.
