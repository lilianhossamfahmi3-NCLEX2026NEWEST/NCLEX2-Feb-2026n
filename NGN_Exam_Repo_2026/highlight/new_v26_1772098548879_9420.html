✓ Clinical Rationales

Maintaining the head of the bed at 30-45 degrees promotes venous drainage from the brain, reducing ICP. This position optimizes cerebral perfusion and minimizes pressure. Administering stool softeners prevents straining during bowel movements (Valsalva maneuver), which can acutely increase ICP. Frequent neurological assessments (e.g., Glasgow Coma Scale, pupillary response) allow for early detection of changes in ICP, guiding timely interventions. A quiet environment reduces external stimuli that can exacerbate ICP by minimizing neuronal excitation and metabolic demand. Limiting stimulation is key to preventing further increases in ICP.
