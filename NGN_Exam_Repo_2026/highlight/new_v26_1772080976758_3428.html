✓ Clinical Rationales

A potassium level of 6.2 mEq/L is critically high and poses an immediate threat to cardiac function. Hyperkalemia can lead to life-threatening arrhythmias such as ventricular fibrillation or asystole. The elevated potassium affects the resting membrane potential of cardiac cells, making them more excitable and prone to dysrhythmias. Immediate intervention is required to shift potassium intracellularly and/or remove it from the body.
