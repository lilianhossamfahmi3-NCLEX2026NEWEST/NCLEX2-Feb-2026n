✓ Clinical Rationales

Hypotension (BP 88/50 mmHg), tachycardia (HR 122 bpm), tachypnea (RR 28 breaths/min), elevated temperature (101.5°F), elevated WBC (18,000/mm3), and elevated lactate (4.0 mmol/L) are all indicative of sepsis and require immediate intervention to prevent further deterioration and potential organ damage. These findings suggest the patient is not perfusing adequately and is in a state of shock. Sepsis is a life-threatening condition caused by the body's overwhelming response to an infection. The body releases chemicals into the bloodstream to fight the infection, but this can trigger widespread inflammation, leading to organ damage and failure. Early recognition and treatment are crucial for improving patient outcomes. The combination of vital sign abnormalities (hypotension, tachycardia, tachypnea, fever) and elevated inflammatory markers (WBC, lactate) strongly suggests sepsis. Immediate interventions include administering IV fluids, antibiotics, and vasopressors if needed, as well as monitoring vital signs and organ function closely.
