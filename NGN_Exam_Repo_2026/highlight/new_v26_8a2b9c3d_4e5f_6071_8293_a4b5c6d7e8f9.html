✓ Clinical Rationales

Septic shock is a life-threatening condition defined by persistent hypotension and cellular metabolic abnormalities indicative of inadequate tissue perfusion. The selected findings—lethargy (cerebral hypoperfusion), hypotension (circulatory collapse), tachycardia and tachypnea (compensatory mechanisms), delayed capillary refill (peripheral vasoconstriction), oliguria (renal hypoperfusion), and elevated lactate (anaerobic metabolism)—are all direct pathophysiological indicators of systemic hypoperfusion and end-organ dysfunction, the hallmarks of shock.
