✓ Clinical Rationales

The highlighted findings indicate digoxin toxicity. Digoxin, a cardiac glycoside, enhances myocardial contractility but has a narrow therapeutic index. This client's age (82) and renal insufficiency significantly increase the risk of toxicity due to decreased drug clearance. Furosemide, a loop diuretic, can cause hypokalemia, further exacerbating digoxin's effects. * **Loss of appetite, persistent nausea, and diarrhea:** These are early gastrointestinal (GI) symptoms of digoxin toxicity. Digoxin affects the GI tract by increasing vagal tone and altering electrolyte balance, leading to these symptoms. * **Seeing yellow-green halos around lights and blurred vision:** These are classic visual disturbances associated with digoxin toxicity. Digoxin affects the retinal cells and optic nerve, causing these characteristic visual changes. * **Apical heart rate of 48 beats/min and irregular:** Digoxin toxicity can cause bradycardia and various arrhythmias. The drug's effect on the sinoatrial (SA) and atrioventricular (AV) nodes can lead to slowed conduction and irregular heart rhythms. * **Feeling extremely weak and tired:** This is a common symptom of digoxin toxicity, resulting from the drug's impact on cellular function and electrolyte imbalances. * **Serum potassium of 3.2 mEq/L:** Hypokalemia increases the binding of digoxin to the Na+/K+ ATPase pump, enhancing its effects and increasing the risk of toxicity. Furosemide contributes to potassium loss. * **Serum digoxin level of 2.8 ng/mL:** This level is above the therapeutic range (0.5-2.0 ng/mL), confirming digoxin toxicity.
