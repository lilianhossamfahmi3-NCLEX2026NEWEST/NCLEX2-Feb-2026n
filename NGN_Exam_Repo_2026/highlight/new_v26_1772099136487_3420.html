✓ Clinical Rationales

A temperature of 101.5°F (38.6°C) in a child receiving chemotherapy is a sign of neutropenic fever, a potentially life-threatening complication. Chemotherapy-induced neutropenia impairs the body's ability to fight infection, making even a mild fever a serious concern. Immediate intervention, including blood cultures and administration of broad-spectrum antibiotics, is required to prevent sepsis and other severe outcomes. The absolute neutrophil count (ANC) is likely very low, increasing the risk of rapid infection progression.
