✓ Clinical Rationales

Accurate monitoring of fluid balance is essential in diabetes insipidus. Desmopressin, a synthetic vasopressin, reduces urine output by mimicking ADH. Daily weights help assess fluid retention or loss, crucial for managing dehydration. Monitoring serum sodium levels is vital as hypernatremia is a common complication due to excessive water loss. Encouraging fluid intake helps prevent dehydration, a primary concern in diabetes insipidus. clinicalPearl: Early recognition and management of fluid imbalance are critical in preventing severe complications in diabetes insipidus.
