✓ Clinical Rationales

Lethargy and confusion, hypotension, rapid respiratory rate with shallow breathing, and right upper quadrant tenderness are all signs of Reye Syndrome progression and require immediate notification of the healthcare provider. Lethargy and confusion indicate worsening encephalopathy due to cerebral edema. Hypotension indicates shock. Rapid respiratory rate with shallow breathing can indicate respiratory failure. Right upper quadrant tenderness can indicate liver inflammation or enlargement.
