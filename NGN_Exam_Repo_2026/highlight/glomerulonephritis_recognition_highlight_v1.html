✓ Clinical Rationales

Acute Post-streptococcal Glomerulonephritis (APSGN) is an inflammatory response in the glomeruli following a recent streptococcal infection (typically pharyngitis or skin infection). The latent period is usually 1-3 weeks. The highlighted findings are consistent with APSGN due to the following pathophysiology: * **'Smoky' or tea-colored urine (hematuria):** Inflammation and damage to the glomerular capillaries cause red blood cells to leak into the urine. * **Periorbital edema:** Decreased GFR leads to fluid retention, initially manifesting as periorbital edema due to the loose tissue around the eyes. * **Hypertension (142/96 mmHg):** Fluid volume overload and activation of the renin-angiotensin-aldosterone system (RAAS) contribute to increased blood pressure. * **Lethargy and Headache:** These symptoms can result from fluid volume excess, electrolyte imbalances, and potential hypertensive encephalopathy. * **Mildly distended abdomen:** Ascites can develop due to fluid shifting into the peritoneal space secondary to decreased oncotic pressure and increased hydrostatic pressure. * **Bilateral 1+ pretibial edema:** Generalized edema occurs as fluid accumulates in the interstitial spaces due to decreased GFR and sodium retention. * **Proteinuria (0.5 to 2.0 g):** Glomerular damage increases permeability, allowing protein to leak into the urine. Note that the proteinuria in APSGN is typically less severe than in nephrotic syndrome. * **Visible hematuria:** Macroscopic blood in the urine, resulting from glomerular capillary damage. * **Decreased glomerular filtration rate (GFR):** Inflammation and obstruction within the glomeruli reduce the kidney's ability to filter waste products. * **Positive antistreptolysin O (ASO) titer:** Indicates a recent streptococcal infection, confirming the etiology of APSGN. * **Weight gain (1.5 kg):** Rapid weight gain over a short period is a strong indicator of fluid retention.
