✓ Clinical Rationales

A fetal heart rate of 110 bpm indicates fetal bradycardia, which can be a sign of fetal distress and requires immediate intervention to ensure adequate oxygenation and prevent potential complications. Fetal bradycardia can be caused by several factors, including maternal hypotension, umbilical cord compression, or fetal hypoxia. Immediate interventions may include repositioning the mother, administering oxygen, and notifying the healthcare provider for further evaluation and potential interventions such as amnioinfusion or expedited delivery. Continuous fetal monitoring is essential to assess the fetal response to interventions.
