The selected findings are direct indicators of fluid volume excess (hypervolemia) secondary to acute decompensated heart failure. The failing heart cannot effectively pump blood forward, leading to backward pressure and fluid accumulation in the pulmonary and systemic circuits. This manifests as crackles, pink frothy sputum, an S3 heart sound, peripheral edema, and rapid weight gain.
