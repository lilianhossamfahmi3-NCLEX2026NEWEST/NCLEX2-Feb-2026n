✓ Clinical Rationales

Asterixis, elevated ammonia level, occasional confusion, and hypotension (BP 90/60) are all critical findings in a client with hepatic encephalopathy. Asterixis and confusion indicate worsening encephalopathy. Elevated ammonia levels directly contribute to the neurological symptoms. Hypotension can indicate hypovolemia or sepsis, both of which can exacerbate liver failure and encephalopathy. These findings require immediate notification to the healthcare provider for potential interventions such as fluid resuscitation, medication adjustments, or further diagnostic testing.
