✓ Clinical Rationales

A hypercyanotic ('tet') spell in Tetralogy of Fallot is a life-threatening emergency caused by a sudden increase in right-to-left shunting of deoxygenated blood into the systemic circulation. This is triggered by a spasm of the right ventricular outflow tract. The correct findings—severe cyanosis, critical hypoxemia (SpO₂ 68%), profound respiratory distress (RR 60 with retractions), and acidosis (pH 7.30)—are direct indicators of this severe physiological decompensation and require immediate intervention to prevent neurological injury or death.
