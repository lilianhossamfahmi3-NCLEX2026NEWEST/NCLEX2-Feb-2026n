✓ Clinical Rationales

In hyperemesis gravidarum, persistent vomiting leads to dehydration, electrolyte imbalances, and nutritional deficiencies. Being unable to keep down food or liquids for 24 hours indicates the severity of the condition and the need for intervention. Minimal and concentrated urine output is a sign of dehydration. Hypotension (BP 90/60 mmHg) is a critical finding suggesting hypovolemia and potential shock, requiring immediate action.
