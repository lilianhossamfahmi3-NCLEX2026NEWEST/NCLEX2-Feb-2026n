✓ Clinical Rationales

The highlighted findings are indicative of compartment syndrome, a serious condition that requires immediate intervention to prevent permanent damage. Severe pain with passive dorsiflexion, pallor, diminished pulse, hypotension, tachycardia, and paresthesia are all key signs. The '6 Ps' are Pain, Pallor, Pulselessness, Paresthesia, Paralysis, and Pressure. The hypotension and tachycardia indicate possible hypovolemic shock secondary to fluid shifts.
