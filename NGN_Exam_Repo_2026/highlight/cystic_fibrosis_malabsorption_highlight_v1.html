✓ Clinical Rationales

Pancreatic insufficiency in cystic fibrosis (CF) results from thick mucus obstructing the pancreatic ducts, preventing digestive enzymes from reaching the small intestine. This leads to malabsorption of fats, proteins, and fat-soluble vitamins. Steatorrhea (frequent, large, greasy, foul-smelling stools that are difficult to flush and contain oil droplets) is a hallmark sign of fat malabsorption. The unabsorbed fat also contributes to abdominal cramping and excessive flatulence. Failure to thrive (weight in the 5th percentile despite a high appetite) and thin, wasted extremities indicate inadequate nutrient absorption. A protuberant abdomen can result from increased gas production and unabsorbed bulk in the intestines. Deficiencies in fat-soluble vitamins (A, D, E, and K) manifest as dry, flaky skin (vitamin A deficiency) and easy bruising (vitamin K deficiency). Digital clubbing is primarily associated with chronic hypoxia due to lung disease in CF, but can also be related to malabsorption.
