Sepsis is a life-threatening condition arising from the body's dysregulated response to an infection. The highlighted findings are indicative of this systemic inflammatory response. A temperature of 102.4°F (39.1°C) signifies fever, a key indicator. A heart rate of 118 bpm indicates tachycardia, often an early sign of sepsis as the heart attempts to compensate for decreased perfusion. A blood pressure of 88/54 mmHg represents hypotension, suggesting inadequate tissue perfusion due to vasodilation and fluid shifts. Warm, flushed skin can be present in early sepsis due to vasodilation. Dark, cloudy urine with a foul odor strongly suggests a urinary tract infection (UTI), a common source of sepsis, especially in older adults. Tachypnea (24 bpm) indicates the body's attempt to compensate for metabolic acidosis and hypoxia.
