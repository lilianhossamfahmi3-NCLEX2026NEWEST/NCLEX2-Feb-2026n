✓ Clinical Rationales

Severe headache, blurred vision, epigastric pain, elevated blood pressure, proteinuria, hyperreflexia with clonus, and decreased urine output are all signs and symptoms of worsening preeclampsia and require immediate notification of the healthcare provider. These findings indicate potential end-organ damage and increased risk for complications such as eclampsia, stroke, HELLP syndrome, and placental abruption.
