✓ Clinical Rationales

Left-sided heart failure (LHF) occurs when the left ventricle cannot effectively pump blood to the systemic circulation, leading to a backup of blood into the pulmonary circulation. This causes increased pulmonary hydrostatic pressure, forcing fluid into the alveoli and interstitial spaces. * **'Three pillows'**: Orthopnea, the need to sit or stand to breathe comfortably, is a classic symptom of LHF. Lying flat increases venous return, exacerbating pulmonary congestion. * **Frothy, pink-tinged sputum**: This indicates pulmonary edema, a severe manifestation of LHF where fluid leaks into the alveoli, mixing with air and potentially some blood. * **Bibasilar crackles**: These adventitious lung sounds are caused by fluid-filled alveoli popping open during inspiration, indicating pulmonary congestion. * **Respiratory rate of 28 breaths per minute**: Tachypnea is a compensatory mechanism to increase oxygen uptake due to impaired gas exchange in the fluid-filled alveoli.
