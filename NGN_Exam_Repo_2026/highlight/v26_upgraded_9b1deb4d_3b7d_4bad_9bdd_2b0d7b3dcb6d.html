✓ Clinical Rationales

The selected findings are classic indicators of fluid volume overload in acute decompensated heart failure. Increased hydrostatic pressure from a failing left ventricle causes fluid to back up into the pulmonary circulation (bibasilar crackles, hypoxemia) and the systemic venous system (JVD, peripheral edema). The body's compensatory mechanisms, like the sympathetic nervous system, increase heart rate and blood pressure. Rapid weight gain is the most sensitive indicator of fluid retention.
