✓ Clinical Rationales

Failure to pass meconium within 48 hours of birth, elevated temperature, tachycardia, tachypnea with grunting, and hypotension are all critical findings in a newborn with suspected Hirschsprung's disease. Failure to pass meconium is a classic sign. Fever indicates possible enterocolitis, a life-threatening complication. Tachycardia, tachypnea, and hypotension are signs of sepsis or shock, requiring immediate intervention.
