✓ Clinical Rationales

Obtain a STAT non-contrast CT scan of the head to rule out hemorrhage. This is the crucial first step to determine if the patient is eligible for thrombolytic therapy (tPA). Rapid identification of the stroke type (ischemic vs. hemorrhagic) is paramount because the treatment pathways diverge significantly. tPA, a potent thrombolytic agent, is indicated for ischemic stroke to dissolve the clot and restore blood flow. However, it is absolutely contraindicated in hemorrhagic stroke, as it would exacerbate the bleeding and worsen the patient's condition.
