✓ Clinical Rationales

The correct findings are direct neurological manifestations of hepatic encephalopathy (HE). HE occurs when the failing liver cannot detoxify ammonia, a neurotoxin, from the blood. Elevated ammonia levels cross the blood-brain barrier, causing cerebral edema and altered neurotransmission. This leads to changes in mental status (confusion, agitation, disorientation), impaired motor function (asterixis), and the presence of fetor hepaticus due to the metabolic byproducts.
